Era I
The Fallen Age
Approx. Years 1–1,200 of the Fallen Age calendar · ending roughly 3,000–4,000 years before the current reckoning
Before the Fallen Age — Deep Time
Nerull begins accumulating
The Old King has been managing the grey between since before any named civilization. He is not new to this. The Fallen Age is one phase in a project of indefinite duration.
Source: Nerull vault file · Doth's Notes throughout Vol. I
Before the Fallen Age — Deep Time
The Litany of the Nine is first performed
Nine champions are bound to Nerull's service in a formal covenant ceremony. They enter willingly. The ninth chamber is left empty — kept for a figure the covenant anticipates but does not yet name.
Source: Vol. II — The Litany of the Nine
Early Fallen Age — approx. Year 760
The Sundering of Vel'Amara
The Conclave of the Open Hand, after twenty-three years of preparation, opens a planar gate. It runs the wrong direction. The city falls silent over three days. One survivor walks out. Vel'Amara is never resettled. Maps mark it: do not approach from the east.
Source: Vol. I — The Sundering of Vel'Amara
Early Fallen Age — approx. Year 800
The Accord of Dravan's Mouth
A Grey Emissary brokers a coalition treaty between seven powers. Terms include a ban on planar research for one hundred years, and the surrender of violators to the emissary for "disposition." Six powers sign. The city-state of Areveth declines and outlasts three signatories.
Source: Vol. I — The Accord of Dravan's Mouth · Diplomatic Register Years 820–880
Year 803 of the Fallen Age
The Three Thrones of Ossian — covenant begins
The Ossian Crown enters formal covenant with the Lord of the Third Account. A third throne — the Steward of the Grey — is established. Ossian receives protection: its dead stay buried, its citizens die once. Nerull receives annual death records in triplicate.
Source: Vol. I — The Three Thrones of Ossian · Ossian Covenant Registers
Year 803–864 of the Fallen Age
The Rite of the First Breath — mandatory in Ossian
Every newborn in Ossian is registered in Nerull's ledger at birth. The rite is warm, parental, and performed within one day. You are written. You are counted. You are not lost between. The claim is filed before the child can speak.
Source: Vol. II — The Rite of the First Breath
Year 864 of the Fallen Age
The Three Thrones of Ossian — covenant completes
The covenant is declared satisfied. The Unnamed Congregation disperses. The third throne is vacated. Three congregation members are found dead at the old hall. Ossian outlasts the covenant by nineteen years before a quality of absence settles over it.
Source: Vol. I — The Three Thrones of Ossian
Year 940–1,091 of the Fallen Age
The Pale Cartographers operate
The Order of the Pale Line surveys the Veil's boundary conditions for over a century. They document thinning sites across the known world, file annual reports with the Ossian Surveyor General's office, and are consistently ignored.
Source: Vol. I — The Pale Cartographers
Approx. Year 1,070 of the Fallen Age
Survey Mark 7 — the Veil goes absent
In the marshlands northeast of what will become the Evergrowing Forest, the Pale Cartographers document a site where the Veil is not thinned but absent. It does not grow. It is held at exactly the same size year after year. A threshold maintained, not a wound spreading. The monitoring station is never built.
Source: Vol. I — The Pale Cartographers · Fragment: The Cartographer's Last Entry
Approx. Year 1,076–1,087 of the Fallen Age
The Unmaking of the Silver Road
Three currency arbiters die in fourteen months — illness, accident, old age. Their replacements negotiate slightly less favorable exchange rates. Eleven years of compound marginal deterioration follow. The last waystation closes in Year 1,087. The postmaster leaves the spare key under a stone in case the road starts up again.
Source: Vol. I — The Unmaking of the Silver Road
Year 1,091 of the Fallen Age
The Pale Cartographers dissolve
The Order votes unanimously to dissolve. Their final resolution: the work we were founded to do has been done, and what happens next is not our work to do. Their records are deposited with the Duskhollow archive. No member appears in any record after this date.
Source: Vol. I — The Pale Cartographers
Shortly after Year 1,091 of the Fallen Age
The Cartographer's Last Entry — Survey Mark 7 revisited
An unnamed Cartographer returns to Survey Mark 7 in violation of the dissolution resolution. The absence is unchanged. Still the same size. Still held. They note the patience at its edge — not like a person waiting, but like a stone. They go home to teach their granddaughter to fish.
Source: Vol. I — Fragment: The Cartographer's Last Entry
Year 1,099 of the Fallen Age
The Last Conclave of Duskhollow
Thirty-one scholars gather under cover of a planar theory colloquium. They compare findings across disciplines. The picture that emerges is not chaos — it is administration. Someone is running a long dissolution. On the final evening, Torvel Ash says plainly what that someone is. He never appears in any record again.
Source: Vol. I — The Last Conclave of Duskhollow
Year 1,101 of the Fallen Age
The Ossuary Institute is founded
Lorekeeper Maren founds the Institute in Duskhollow. Her stated purpose: to ensure that what happened is understood by those who come after. She is building the archive for whoever comes after the interval ends. She knows she will not be the one to use it.
Source: Vol. I — The Last Conclave of Duskhollow
Approx. Year 1,200 of the Fallen Age
The Collapse — the Fallen Age ends
The Age does not end dramatically. It has been ending for a century. The collapse is the moment when the last functioning institutions cease to function. What it leaves behind: the Duskhollow archive, the Ossuary Institute, Survey Mark 7, and the grey between — louder now, and patient.
Source: Vol. I — Editor's Preface (Doth)
The Long Interval
~3,000 to 4,000 Years of Silence
The Fallen Age ends. The world rebuilds slowly, without memory of what came before. Nerull consolidates. The grey between stabilizes. Survey Mark 7 remains open — held at exactly the same size, by whatever holds it, for millennia. The Ossuary Institute endures, changes hands, and eventually becomes something Maren would not recognize.
Era II
The Current Reckoning — Years of the Greying
The calendar of the present era · the Ossuary Institute persists in altered form
Year of the Greying 1–10
Ossuary Institute archive recovery begins
The Institute, now generations removed from Maren's founding, begins systematic recovery of the Blackened Testimonies. The first expeditions to Vis'Avae recover multiple shards. The archive Maren built has survived the interval. The scholars working it do not yet understand what they have.
Source: Vol. III — Editor's Preface (Aldric Senne)
Year of the Greying 11
The Cartographer's Last Entry is found
A loose document is recovered from the Duskhollow archive, unsorted, inside a sealed leather case bearing the Pale Line guild mark. The handwriting matches no known Cartographer. It describes Survey Mark 7. It is filed and largely ignored for seventeen years, until Doth finds it.
Source: Vol. I — Fragment: The Cartographer's Last Entry
Year of the Greying 14
Silver Road trade records compiled
The Duskhollow Institute of Commerce produces a compilation of surviving trade registry records from multiple city archives. The Silver Road's collapse is documented for the first time as a unified event rather than a series of local failures.
Source: Vol. I — The Unmaking of the Silver Road
Year of the Greying 22
The Binding Words of Vel'Amara are recovered
Archivist-Field Tomas Renn leads an expedition to the Vel'Amara site and recovers a stone box from a sealed sub-basement. The box was locked from the outside. The key was in the lock. Inside: the Binding Words — a planar interface rite with two sections missing. The Compact and Closing are absent.
Source: Vol. II — The Binding Words of Vel'Amara
Year of the Greying 28–33
Serevane Doth compiles Vol. I — Histories of the Fallen Age
Doth, First Archivist, spends five years building the historical volume. He finds the Cartographer's Last Entry, reads the Pale Cartographers' full archive, traces Nerull's operational presence across six historical events, and becomes increasingly certain of something he cannot bring himself to name plainly in print.
Source: Vol. I — Editor's Preface and Closing Note
Year of the Greying 33 — after Vol. I is published
Doth writes the Prayer of the Third Door
GM only. After reading the liturgical material designated for Vol. II, Doth understands the full shape of what he has been working inside. He writes the Prayer for himself and hides it inside the Pale Cartographers' final survey report — the Survey Mark 7 document. He addresses it to whatever is on the other side of the threshold. He says he is not afraid. He means it.
Source: DM Notes — Ashen Codex Hidden Architecture
Year of the Greying 34–39
Auren Vestl compiles Vol. II — Liturgies of the Fallen Age
Vestl, Second Archivist, spends five years on the liturgical material. She finds the Prayer of the Third Door among the archive's unsorted materials. She recognizes Doth's voice. She does not write down whose it is. She finishes the volume he assigned her and addresses her closing note to whoever comes after.
Source: Vol. II — Editor's Preface and Closing Note
Year of the Greying 36
Serevane Doth dies at his desk
GM only. Doth is found dead mid-sentence, working on Vol. IV — the cosmological material, Nerull's domain, the records of what the Fallen Age believed waited at the end of the third door. The pen is still in his hand. Vestl becomes Second Archivist the following month.
Source: Vol. II — Editor's Closing Note (Vestl)
Year of the Greying 41–44
Aldric Senne compiles Vol. III — Prophecies of the Fallen Age
Senne, twenty-seven years old and certain he does not believe in prophecy, spends three years with the Blackened Testimonies. He becomes a man who writes "I suspect the latter" and "I find that I cannot say." Vol. III is published in Year 44. It is the last honest work the Ossuary Institute produces.
Source: Vol. III — Editor's Preface and Closing Note
Year of the Greying 39 — Vis'Avae fire
Shard 5 of the Blackened Testimonies is destroyed
A fire at the Vis'Avae archive destroys Shard 5 — reportedly the longest single fragment in the collection. Two expedition members who had briefly handled it disagreed on what it concerned. One said water. The other disagreed. The text is unrecoverable.
Source: Vol. III — Table of Contents notation
Year of the Greying 51
The Ossuary Institute dissolves — becomes a death cult
Seven years after Vol. III is published, the Institute votes to redirect its mission from historical preservation toward active liturgical practice. The minority who object leave. The majority who stay gradually become what the documents they were preserving had warned against. They keep excellent records. They sing the Canticle of the Kept. They are the kept, and they are grateful.
Source: DM Notes — Ashen Codex Hidden Architecture
Year of the Greying 51 — noted
Vol. IV is never written
The cosmological material — Nerull's domain, the records of what the Fallen Age believed waited at the end of the third door — remains unsorted on Doth's old desk. No archivist returns to it. The Ossuary Institute's successor institution has no interest in documenting what it now worships.
Source: Vol. I and Vol. III — Closing Notes
Era III
The Present — The Veil's Whisper
Current campaign timeline · Session 14 onward
Approx. 1,600 years before present
Xaphan is trapped beyond the Veil
The fire genasi Xaphan — who will eventually want to die — is bound beyond Nerull's Veil. His imprisonment begins during the long interval following the Fallen Age's collapse. He has been waiting longer than most civilizations have existed.
Source: Campaign vault — Oracle dialogue
Indeterminate — deep past
Talia fills the ninth chamber
GM only. The slot left empty in the Litany of the Nine is eventually filled. Talia enters the covenant willingly, in full knowledge of its terms. The covenant anticipated her before she existed. She thinks she has been running her own game. She has been filling a pre-cut slot in someone else's.
Source: DM Notes · Vol. II — The Litany of the Nine
Present — Arc 7 onward
The party carries the Crown toward Samantha's coronation
The wolves are herded. They carry a key of bone — Nasha's sternum, buried in Vis'Avae. They carry a blessing for the hand that leads — Xaphan's beating heart, which the party will harvest unknowingly. They are prepared to die. The preparation is the point. The Fallen Age's long setup is reaching its conclusion.
Source: Vol. III — Prophecy XIV · Campaign arc notes
Present — Survey Mark 7
The threshold is still open
GM only. Survey Mark 7, documented by the Pale Cartographers millennia ago as an absent section of the Veil held open at a fixed size, is located in the marshlands northeast of the Evergrowing Forest — the Whispering Asylum region, or adjacent to it. It has been held open across the entire interval. The party has not found it yet.
Source: DM Notes — Ashen Codex Hidden Architecture
