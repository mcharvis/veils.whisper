# The Long Grey

**Date:** TBD
**Location:** Christine's House
**DM:** Mike
**Players Present:** Ryan, Tristan, Christine, Mike Charvis

---

## Introduction
> Prepare to die.
> 
> The Pedronian Empire is a graveyard of resurrection. You stand in the wreckage of a single, bloody night where the air tastes like ash and the gods themselves have been starved of their worship.
> 
> It began in the mind of Kovinar, where Samantha attempted to dismantle the paladin’s faith. She mocked his devotion to Bahamut, labeled his "Good" as a fear of power, and laid the blood of Nasha at hit feet. She handed over the Six Commands of Pedronia, told you to not ask what follows, and left you with the cold truth: your choices were an illusion maintained by Nerull.
> 
> You survived the cold steel of Elder Susanoo and Goku not through strength, but through Renzo’s acceptance of failure. You commanded the clans to stand down with the authority of the grave, and they obeyed. You navigated the pagodas of Raijin and Fujin, and you watched Renzo break under the radiant wrath of Sovereign Amaterasu. He died, only to be snatched back by Caelen to rule the ruins as a living reliquary. With the gods Amaterasu and Tsukuyomi faded into ash, the empire finally knew its master.
> 
> In the Hikarai, you took the tsukaru from the pikes of thirteen severed heads and put down a Death Knight. Upon exiting, Wilson died for several minutes, only to wake with a second, unknown heartbeat drumming in his chest...a rhythm that you can't place.
> 
> Then came Malphas...Samantha's cruel prediction. A silhouette of horns and wings that dismantled you with ease, sealing Kovinar in a sacrificial tower and demanding the stone. He promised a swift demise for your surrender and eternal agony for your defiance.
> 
> He didn't count on Samantha. She tore through the stone of the tower, mocked your prayers, and labeled your combat as "shit." With runes of blood and shadow, she turned you into god hunters. She disintegrated the mind of Malphas while Kovinar took its head.
> 
> Now, the aftermath is silent. Nerull has claimed the remains of Malphas. Samantha has taken the Book of the Profane Word and walked away. The Oracle tells you that she can no longer see her connection to Elarith. She is disconnected from the tapestry of this world, a wildcard loose in a land that is already burning.
> 
> The Tsukaru is in your hands, a second heart is beating in the chest of a zombie rat, and the only person who can save you cannot be tracked. You are in desperate need for guidance and hopefully prepared to die.

## 🧭 Summary

> The [[06_NPCs/Talia\|Oracle]] led the party to a hidden octagonal mausoleum of void-black basalt deep in the forest — the tomb of the **[[Knights of Nine\|Knights of Nine]]**. Eight sarcophagi lined the walls, lids pushed aside. The inscription carved into the altar warned of nine champions chained to [[06_NPCs/Gods/Nerull\|Nerull]] in iron — but only eight were entombed. The ninth walked free. The Oracle's hands shook. The first time the party had ever seen her afraid.
>
> She revealed she had walked the **[[01_Campaigns/Encounters/6 - The Long Grey/The Long Grey\|Long Grey]]** once before alongside [[05_Player Characters/Inactive/Xaphan\|Xaphan]], and failed — lacking the mental fortitude to accept her own trauma. He had left her behind because he had to. *"Do not carry that which you cannot afford to lose,"* she warned, and sent them through.
>
> The party stood before the **Threshold of the Unadorned**, where their legendary steel and storied gear became unbearable weights against their souls. They laid their identities in the Basin of Relinquishment and crossed that fragile bridge of glass — realizing that a hero who is nothing without their blade is nothing at all.
>
> They leaned into the **Sullen Wind**, planting their feet as it moaned with the weight of every unfair moment they'd ever endured. They walked through the **Archive of Regret** — past the weeping spirits of [[06_NPCs/Nasha\|Nasha]] and the tiefling women, past the old gang and the womb-born griefs that haunt their sleep — and did not reach back into the rift to fix the past. They looked into the Mirror of the Present, weary and scarred, and chose the cold, bare facts of reality. [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]] buckled to selfishness at a fountain that promised life, discovering who pays for it.
>
> At the **Great Confluence**, they faltered. They saw [[06_NPCs/Kaelin Vetch\|Kaelin Vetch]] slipping into the lava abyss and reached for the falling leaf — pouring magic into a story already written. He fell anyway. The universe took its tithe: a permanent -1 to Wisdom (Insight) for all who reached. They carried that weight into the **Court of the Unjust**, where [[06_NPCs/Malphas\|Malphas]], [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Rauconen]], and [[06_NPCs/Malis Fiani, The Scarlet Raven\|Samantha]] were crowned in gold while the party sat in chains — and remained in their Inner Citadel, refusing to rage. They chose the Paternoster over the Ladder of Thorns and narrowly avoided being drowned in the **Mirror of the Final Breath**.
>
> Then came the final dismantling of the Ego.
>
> They walked past the **Hall of the Gilded Return** without reclaiming their gear — naked, vulnerable, walking. They turned their backs on the portal home in the **Archive of Impossible Odds**, choosing the 0.01% over the certainty of safety. They used the golden key in the **Hall of the Broken Mirror** to free their antagonists rather than strike them. When the stars called them Saints in the **Threshold of the Void**, they murdered their own pride.
>
> Finally — the **Collapse of the Firmament**. The Third Shard of the [[Items/Magical/Death-Wrought Mosaic\|Death-Wrought Mosaic]] hovered over jagged obsidian in an infinite violet nebula. The roar of the void made coordination impossible. Each chose alone, in silence. Not one reached with a clenched fist. They knelt. They offered themselves as stewards, not thieves.
>
> The pillar melted like wax. [[06_NPCs/Demigods/The Three Sisters/Hecate\|Hecate]]'s voice filled the silence: *"You own nothing. Not your gear, not your name, not even your life. And because you have let go of everything, you are finally strong enough to carry this."*
>
> The Third Shard drifted into waiting palms. The [[Items/The Tsukaru\|Tsukaru]] was surrendered to [[06_NPCs/Demigods/The Three Sisters/Hecate\|Hecate]] in exchange — transactional, no ceremony, nothing offered in return but the trade itself.
>
> Then [[06_NPCs/Demigods/The Three Sisters/Hecate\|Hecate]] placed a cold hand over [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]]'s eyes. A vision: [[05_Player Characters/Varian\|Varian]] at the threshold of the mausoleum, eyes milky white and leaking purple light, chanting in grinding static — opening the tombs of the Knights of Nine. Mercenaries in dark iron following him through a hidden mountain door. Somewhere ahead, [[06_NPCs/Demigods/The Three Sisters/Nyx\|Nyx]] was waiting.

## 🔍 Clues & Lore
- **The Ninth Knight** — the inscription reads: *"The ninth remains free and stays alive to wake the rest so none survive."* Only eight are entombed. The ninth is operating freely in the world.
- **The Oracle failed the Long Grey** — she walked it alongside Xaphan, lacked the fortitude to continue, and was left behind. *"He left me in the Grey to be his way back."* She made a promise to guide the party.
- **Xaphan's prophecy (from the Oracle)** — Xaphan rambled in the Long Grey about mortals fulfilling a prophecy that would untangle the threads of Fate. In that moment of liberation, his lifeforce would become bound to the Oracle's. *"For eternity, his path would remain connected to mine, the hearth and the home, finally free of the Reaper's hold."*
- **Hecate's trade** — the Tsukaru for the Third Shard. She did not explain what she intends to do with it. 
- **Varian's possession** — milky white eyes, oily purple light leaking from eyes and mouth, chanting in grinding static. He opened the mausoleum, then pursued by mercenaries through a hidden mountain door in the woods. This is not Varian's magic — something is using him.
