#organization

The Pale Flame did not start as anyone's weapon. Its founders, generations gone now, believed something almost beautiful: that suffering, carried honestly and without complaint, could temper a soul the way fire tempers steel. Pain endured in silence, they taught, earns a redemption that pain inflicted on others never could. Novices walked barefoot across ash and ruin. They whispered rites over the unburied dead. They took on the wounds of murderers as penance offered on another's behalf, and forgave, without flinching, men the rest of the world would have hanged twice over.

Every initiate is given a candle at ordination, lit once and never relit, kept burning in the order's inner sanctum for as long as they hold their vows. Their dead aren't buried so much as recorded, a single line in the Ash Registry, the order's accounting of every soul who ever wore the flame, faithful or fallen. To be struck from it, candle shattered, name erased, is the only punishment the Pale Flame considers worse than death. They have a word for what's left of a person afterward. Broken Flame.

Somewhere between that founding and now, the doctrine curdled. Suffering nobly borne became suffering imposed, on others, for their own good, the same way the Sanctifiers tell themselves every cruelty is salvation. The two orders found each other the way doctrines like that always do, and in the rotting city of Basville, monks who once whispered comfort to the dying instead burned children alive in locked basements, calling it sacrifice to a god too stillborn to ever object. Members of the Noble House of Vaer-Lorne knew. Some helped.

The alliance reaches further than Basville. In Jade Stronghold, [[06_NPCs/Councilor Varn Drest\|Councilor Varn Drest]] plays the reformer in public and bleeds Oasis coin to the [[04_Organizations/The Sanctifiers\|Sanctifiers]] in private, a double agent the Pale Flame is patient enough never to rush. He doesn't wear a candle. He has never set foot in the inner sanctum. That's rather the point of him.

Not everyone in the order looked away. [[06_NPCs/Seren of the Pale Flame\|Seren]] did not. She broke an altar with her bare hands, killed a cardinal who'd helped build it, and walked six children out of Basville through smoke and ash before anyone could stop her. The order excommunicated her for it, the way an institution always punishes the member who proves its doctrine was never really about mercy. They call her Broken Flame now, and they don't speak her name where novices can hear it.

But some, quietly, in the places novices can't hear either, say her candle is still burning.
