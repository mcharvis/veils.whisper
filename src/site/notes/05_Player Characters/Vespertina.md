When her father, the kind and respected [[06_NPCs/King Aldenor\|King Aldenor]] of [[The Elven Empire of Elarion\|The Elven Empire of Elarion]] died, the young Princess Vespertina Galanodel was next in line to inherit the throne. However, her uncle, [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Lord Rauconen]], convinced the [[04_Organizations/Royal Council\|Royal Council]] that he should assume the role of Regent and safeguard the Crown until she was old enough to rule. Though Vespertina had never found him warm, Rauconen was once beloved by the kingdom and honored as a war hero. But it wasn't long before he became consumed by greed, and his true colors—those of ruthless tyranny—came to light. He allied with wretched leaders, betrayed former allies, slaughtered his own people, and transformed a once-noble army into a cruel and merciless force that devastated neighboring kingdoms—leaving their people either silenced or enslaved.

Vespertina, sheltered but not blind, knew in her heart that her uncle had murdered her father. But with eyes constantly watching her, she could do little more than endure—wearing her father's cloak just to feel close to the man he had been. One evening, as she stood at a window overlooking the city, cloaked in her father's memory, fate intervened. A rogue spell struck her. It was cast by [[Sorceress Sylastra, the Grieving\|a sorceress]] whose village had been destroyed by [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Rauconen]]'s forces. Mistaking the royal-cloaked figure in the window for Rauconen himself, the sorceress had snuck into the castle and unleashed a curse forged in fury and grief.

Vespertina sensed the attack too late. Her own budding sorcery flared instinctively, but it could only blunt the spell, not stop it. The transformation was slow and agonizing: scales crept across her skin, claws replaced her hands, and reptilian eyes glowed in the dark. Horrified by what she had done, the sorceress fled.

Vespertina had been cursed by hatred meant for her tyrannical uncle—transformed into a beast of ancient and monstrous power. Fearing discovery, she fled the castle under cover of night, still wrapped in her father's cloak. She wandered far until she found refuge with a kind, solitary [[Maela, The Innkeeper\|innkeeper]] who asked few questions and offered her shelter.

Since then, Vespertina has journeyed across the land, strengthening her magic and seeking to undo the damage [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Rauconen]] has wrought. She studies the arcane, learns the ways of the world and its people, and hones her skills in battle. Her goal: to one day return, defeat her uncle, reclaim her rightful throne, and restore the kingdom her father once ruled with honor.



[[The Order of the Gilded Flame\|The Order of the Gilded Flame]] – Loyalist knights scattered after [[06_NPCs/King Aldenor\|Aldenor]]'s fall; some still serve in secret.

### Allies
[[Maela, The Innkeeper\|Maela, The Innkeeper]] – Gave Vespertina shelter when she fled the castle; quiet but resourceful.
Ser Calven Dros – Knight of the Gilded Flame, secretly gathering support for her return.

### Enemies
[[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Lord Rauconen]] – Her uncle, the usurper king, who rules through tyranny and fear.
[[The Red Vultures\|The Red Vultures]] – Elite enforcers of [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Rauconen]]'s regime, known for brutality.
Sylastra, the Grieving – Sorceress who accidentally cursed Vespertina; her current motives are unclear.

## Quick Reference — Active Gear & Marks

**[[Dragonguard\|Dragonguard]]**
- +1 bonus to AC while worn.
- Advantage on saving throws against the breath weapons of creatures with the dragon type.

**[[Items/Magical/Veiled Necrovitalis\|Veiled Necrovitalis]]** (attuned)
- Soul Battery: store up to 3 souls; expend one to regain a used spell slot of level 3 or lower.
- Unending Legion: if you control fewer undead than your Charisma modifier, the relic animates additional nearby corpses until you reach that limit.
- Necrotic Surge (reaction): undead within 30 ft. add 1d8 necrotic damage to their next attack.
- Already imbued with *Spare the Dying* and *Animate Dead*.

**[[Items/Staff of the Corpsefield\|Staff of the Corpsefield]]**
- Harvest Life (reaction): when a creature within 30 ft. dies, gain temp HP equal to its CR or level.
- Grave Conduit (action): impale the staff into the ground — for 1 minute, undead you control within 30 ft. grant +2 AC and +2 to damage rolls on your next turn.
- Sepulchral Veil (1/long rest, reaction): when targeted by an attack, become incorporeal until the start of your next turn, taking half damage from all sources until then.

**[[Items/Magical/Cinderflare Wayfinder\|Cinderflare Wayfinder]]** (attuned)
- 1/long rest: Inscribe Waypoint (mark your current location), then Teleport back to it from any distance on the same plane.

**Nerull's Bargains** (Trial of Nerull, permanent)
- Doubt's Edge: gain advantage on your first attack roll, saving throw, or ability check each combat. Cost: your Initiative roll is always made with disadvantage.
- Wall of Force: cast once per long rest, no spell slot required. Cost: forgot *Seeming* (previously known spell of equal or higher level).

**Mark: Echo of Power** — Once per long rest, when you cast a spell of 1st level or higher, you may immediately cast it again without expending a slot.
