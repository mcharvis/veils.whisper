## Introduction
> Prepare to die.
>
> The air in the forest was stagnant, smelling of centuries of weeping rain and the "rotting teeth" of leaning headstones. You followed the Oracle, her frame a splintered silhouette, into that octagonal vault of void-black basalt. Inside, you read the warning of the **[[Knights of Nine\|Knights of Nine]]**—the stars chained in iron to feed on blood—and realized the chilling truth: *For every one is a mirror of you.*
>
> The Oracle's hands shook as she directed you into the **[[01_Campaigns/Encounters/6 - The Long Grey/The Long Grey\|Long Grey]]**, a monochrome nightmare where she walked alongside [[05_Player Characters/Inactive/Xaphan\|Xaphan]]...until she could walk no further...choosing to fail her own quest.
>
> "Do not carry that which you cannot afford to lose," she warned.
>
> You stood before the **Threshold of the Unadorned**, where your legendary steel and storied gear became unbearable weights against your soul. You laid your very identities in the Basin of Relinquishment, crossing that fragile bridge of glass — realizing that a hero who is nothing without their blade is nothing at all.
>
> You leaned into the **Sullen Wind**, planting your feet as it moaned with the weight of every unfair moment you've ever endured. You walked through the **Archive of Regret**, past the weeping spirits of [[06_NPCs/Nasha\|Nasha]] and the tiefling women, past the old gang and the womb-born griefs that haunt your sleep. You did not reach back into the rift to fix the past. You looked into the Mirror of the Present, weary and scarred, and chose the cold, bare facts of reality. [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]] buckled to selfishness in a fountain that promised life—discovering who pays for it.
>
> But the Grey is a thresher of the spirit. At the **Great Confluence**, you faltered. You saw [[06_NPCs/Kaelin Vetch\|Kaelin Vetch]] slipping into the abyss and you reached for the falling leaf. You poured your magic into a story already written, and for that Heroic Delusion, the universe took its tithe. You diminished your own sight to fight the inevitable, and you carried that weight into the **Court of the Unjust**, where you watched [[06_NPCs/Malphas\|Malphas]], [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Rauconen]], and [[06_NPCs/Malis Fiani, The Scarlet Raven\|Samantha]] crowned in gold while you sat in chains. You remained in your Inner Citadel, realizing that a villain's gold is but a shiny pebble.
>
> You refused to be flayed by the **Ladder of Thorns**, choosing rather to be preserved in the velvet lie of the Paternoster. You narrowly avoided being drowned in the **Mirror of the Final Breath**.
>
> Then came the end. The final dismantling of the Ego.
>
> You walked past the **Hall of the Gilded Return**, refusing to reclaim your gear even as you stood naked and vulnerable. You stood before the **Archive of Impossible Odds** and saw the "0.01%" chance of success hanging like a death sentence—and you turned your back on the portal home. You walked past the cages of your antagonists, refusing to strike the men who ruined your lives, choosing *righteousness* over retribution. Even when the stars cheered for you as Saints, you murdered your own pride, whispering that you were merely tools of Nature doing a man's work.
>
> Finally, you stood upon that disk of white stone, suspended in the void. The **Third Shard** hovered before you. Coordination was impossible. The roar of the nebula drowned out every spell and signal. You were alone in your own minds. Not one of you reached out with a clenched fist. You knelt. You offered yourselves as stewards, not thieves.
>
> The obsidian pillar melted like wax.
>
> *"You own nothing,"* [[06_NPCs/Demigods/The Three Sisters/Hecate\|Hecate]]'s voice echoed. *"Not your gear, not your name, not even your life. And because you have let go of everything, you are finally strong enough to carry this."*
>
> You are broken. You are exhausted. You have traded the [[Items/The Tsukaru\|Tsukaru]] into the cold palms of a goddess who knows no blessing nor mercy. But as the Shard drifted into your waiting hands, you realized the final lesson: if the Fates shall find it necessary, you are all prepared to die.

## 🧭 Summary

> The descent into the **Blackmire** began immediately after Hecate's vision faded — a lightless underground passage carved by veins of emerald and amethyst light, reeking of coal and graveyard dust. At the base of the stairs, the party found **Garrik** — a dying mercenary of the Black Wolf's crew, slumped against a boulder, his armor caked in filth and a wound weeping dark, sluggish red. He had followed [[05_Player Characters/Varian\|Varian]] into these woods after watching him open a hidden mountain door with purple fire no one recognized as his. The rest of the Black Wolf's crew had been taken by the mire. Garrik was the last.
>
> He gave them what he knew: [[05_Player Characters/Varian\|Varian]] had gone hunting for **[[06_NPCs/Demigods/The Three Sisters/Nyx\|Nyx]]** — *"a real dark one, a cruel one"* — deep in the gut of the woods. A pack of followers crawled in the dirt around her, worshipping the dark she brought. The Black Wolf had taken Varian and a Crown he had found, and was heading for the **Sleepy Griffin Inn** to meet a buyer. *"He's already a corpse,"* Garrik said, *"he just hasn't stopped moving yet."* He asked to be buried deep. They obliged.
>
> The first trial of the Sanguine Trail was sound. The party ascended a gallery of crucified mercenaries — mouths stitched shut with black thread — where any noise caused the Quiet to lash back. **Two Invisible Stalkers** used shove to force noise. A **Banshee** lurked at the rear, waiting for the DC to climb. A **Stone Golem** slowed passage through the dead zone. They survived through silence and discipline.
>
> The second trial was sight. The **Cinder-Gorge** — a narrow, lightless passage where thousands of black threads hung from the ceiling, each weighted by a plucked eyeball, and caustic Cinder-Mist filled the air with necrotic ink. The party navigated blindfolded, describing intent rather than pointing at maps. **Ropers** on the ceiling pulled players upward toward the basalt. **Hook Horrors** hunted by echolocation. The temptation to peek was constant. The cost was 4d10 necrotic and a lingering curse.
>
> The third trial was breath. The **Breathless Ascent** — a 200-foot vertical chimney of basalt filled with pressurized ground glass and necrotic ink. No inhaling. Every round cost breath points. Every slip risked an automatic gasp and 6d10 necrotic damage. Three **Invisible Stalkers** shoved climbers into each other. A **Ghost** used Horrifying Visage to accelerate breath loss. [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]] and [[05_Player Characters/Squeaks\|Squeaks]] each inhaled once before cresting the ledge.
>
> At the top — the **Mirror in the Graveyard**. Members of the [[Knights of Nine\|Knights of Nine]] manifested from the shadow — black ichor weeping from every joint in their armor, pooling and reabsorbing at their feet. The leader, a distortion of a paladin, spoke without breath: *"Relinquish the Crown."* A Shadow-Thief drifted through the party's ranks, checking satchels with cold phantom fingers. Finding nothing, it withdrew. A vulture descended and collapsed into smoke, and from that smoke stepped a Druid. The paladin asked who had the Crown. *"The Black Wolf,"* the Druid whispered. *"And the summoner? A pawn — once in their ranks. Varian."* The Knights drew blades of solidified smoke. Their warning: abandon the crown, remain among the quiet, or be stitched into the tapestry of the forgotten. Then they dissolved — one by one — back into oily dark. A lone raven on a branch behind the party gave a single caw and flew off.
>
> The silence that followed was broken by [[06_NPCs/Pets/Wilson\|Wilson]].
>
> A violent pulse of emerald light detonated from inside him — jagged and strobing, turning the forest into a green-washed nightmare. Everything within ten feet jerked upward: dust, leaves, pebbles, skeletons, Wilson himself — suspended in a frozen, weightless dance. [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]] felt a fist close inside their chest and collapsed to the dirt, gasping. [[05_Player Characters/Squeaks\|Squeaks]] felt a cold yank deep behind her sternum — a thread pulled taut, something massive and unseen pulling Wilson, anchored only by her soul. Then the world slammed back. Gravity returned. Wilson hit the ground, lay motionless — and then rolled over, shook dust from his fur, and began grooming a loose suture on his leg as if the laws of reality hadn't just buckled around him. The air within ten feet of him smelled faintly of ancient books and rotten ink.
