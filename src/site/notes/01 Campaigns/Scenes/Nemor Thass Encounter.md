A voice snaps through the door:  
“More zombies?! FUCK! When can I catch a break!”

Nemor grits his teeth but keeps his hand pressed to the wood. He doesn’t ask for help. He doesn’t flinch.

“I was expecting them, not you. But maybe you’ll be more interesting.”

_(When the party eventually breaches the door, they find Nemor in Halric’s ruined office—books torn, the walls scorched with sigils, and something **unfinished** on the table: a binding circle, mid-script. He hasn’t been hiding. He’s been **defending the room**.)_

“Before we dance blades and break spells, let me guess… you’ve come to mourn the Sanctifiers? Or judge them? Or maybe loot their bodies like the rest.”

_(He smirks.)_  
“Maelth would’ve fit in with you. Ran like a coward during the siege. He deserves worse than death, and I intend to give it to him myself.”

_(His expression hardens. His voice lowers.)_  
“But first—I’ll deal with you.”
