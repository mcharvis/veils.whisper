## What the Party Knows

They saw Conquest take him.

They were already broken and bleeding when the Rider reached down and seized Lucian by the breastplate and threw him across the warhorse like stolen property and vanished into the smoke. They saw it happen. They could not stop it. They collapsed in the ruin shortly after, and the last image some of them carried into unconsciousness was a knight disappearing into the dark on the back of something that does not return what it takes.

They carried that through everything that followed. 

When they saw Lucian again, they had no idea what he paid and gained for freedom...and how he may have singlehandedly opened the only path to reunite the clans of the Pedronian Empire...a quest the party won't be given for a long time.

---

The place Conquest brought him had no walls, no sky, no ground. It was a condition rather than a location — an atmosphere of dominion. Around him in whatever served as distance were the artifacts of aeons of the Rider's work: broken treaties pressed flat like dried flowers, surrendered standards stacked in cairns, the accumulated debris of every pact that had failed the moment the cost became clear.

Conquest was studying Lucian.

It had taken him because the party had something it wanted and Lucian was the thing it could reach — but it had also taken him because he was interesting. A man who had made a voluntary oath. Four hundred years of service to a betrayed dead man who had lost not only command but a legacy. The Rider moved among the artifacts of broken oaths and had rarely encountered one that had held this long, this completely, for this little tangible reward.

It wanted to understand the mechanism of that. It wanted to find the point at which the oath would crack.

It found the crack immediately. It was in a tiefling named Kovinar.

---

Conquest did not threaten Lucian directly. It did not need to. It simply showed him what was happening — not in images, not in visions, but in the specific way that a force of nature can... It makes a mortal understand the shape of the world at a given moment: the party, broken and scattered, navigating the consequences of a battle they had barely survived. Moving toward the First World Tree without him. His brother...moving towards the unknown, and he was not there to protect him.

He had subconsciously aligned with Kovinar despite knowing it contradicted his oath to Caelen.

That was the crack. Conquest pressed it.

Not with cruelty. With patience. With the specific, unemotional pressure of something that has all the time in the universe and knows exactly which wound to lean on. It offered nothing. It promised nothing. It simply made him understand, with increasing clarity, that every hour he spent here was an eternity the party spent without him moving towards an unknown fate.

Lucian did not break. But he began to think.

---

He may have had something Conquest might want.

He had spent four centuries as a witness. He had fought there — in the specific catastrophe of Caelen's final act. He had watched the Sovereigns move. He had seen what Amaterasu and Tsukuyomi did to a man who had unified six clans without divine sanction, who had demonstrated that mortals did not require gods to maintain order. He had been close enough to Caelen's fall to carry it in his body like an old injury — not as information, but as knowledge. The difference between knowing a thing and having been there.

He offered it to Conquest.

*I offer a path to the demigods that betrayed a forgotten hero.*

He believed he was offering intelligence. He believed he was giving away a piece of the past — painful, irreplaceable, but survivable. He had the history. He had the oath. He had Caelen's name pressed into his own mind like a brand.  

Conquest was still for a long moment.

What Lucian did not understand — what he could not have understood — was what his oath had been doing for four hundred years. It was not simply a memorial. It was a protection. The pact he had held in the Tomb of the Forgotten Hero, the specific structure of a knight's vow sustained across centuries by genuine love and genuine grief, had been holding something in place over the Pedronian Empire. Caelen's spirit was alive...somewhere. It had remained quietly remembered by the only people still actively honoring him...until hundreds of years later when Renzo will sacrifice himself to honor his family and arrive in Elarith.

By surrendering the knowledge/memory of Caelen's fall and the oath honoring him, Lucian was not giving Conquest information about the Sovereigns. He was releasing the last Elarithan tether on the protection Caelen had built. Without knowing it, he was withdrawing the shield that had been standing between Caelen's spirit and the sovereign's complete dominion of Pedronia.

Conquest accepted the trade, not recognizing that the oath to Caelen was still barely alive in an undead Japanese samurai named Renzo. 

So, being stripped of his oath, the knight made a new one. To Kovinar; to Bahamut.

---

The memory left him cleanly. That was the worst part of it. He had expected it to feel like tearing — had steeled himself for the specific grief of losing something that had mattered — but it went the way and exhale goes...without ceremony.

He remembered Caelen. He remembered the campaigns, the brotherhood, the specific quality of a man who had become unforgotten and did it without self-pity. He remembered the past four hundred years, and...unexpectedly, remembered Kovinar's hand on his shoulder and the Cursed Cross flaring gold.

What he did not remember anymore was the fall and his oath to honor it. He didn't remember the night the Sovereigns acted....the brevity of what Amaterasu and Tsukuyomi had done, and how they had done it, and what it had looked like from where he was standing.

It was simply gone. He did not know what he had actually given.

Conquest released him without ceremony. He landed in the Evergrowing Forest and stood for a moment in the specific disorientation of a man who has just paid a price and cannot fully account for it. Something was different. He could feel the shape of the difference without being able to name it — a lightness where there had been weight, an opening where there had been structure...a spiritual tailwind created by an oath to a platinum dragon.

He did not examine it. There was no time. Kovinar was ahead of him, moving toward the doom of the First World Tree, and he was not going to abandon him now.

He started running...through  [[The Evergrowing Forest\|The Evergrowing Forest]], through the [[Vindicative Enterprises\|Vindicative Enterprises]], and into the hellish roots of [[02 World/The First World Tree\|The First World Tree]].

---

The protection Lucian's oath had provided over the Pedronian Empire — the specific, accidental shielding that four centuries of devoted memory had sustained without anyone intending it — dissolved the moment the trade was completed.

What had been held in stone could now move.

Caelen's soul, suspended in the Chamber of the Fallen Flame, had not been trapped by the Sovereigns. It had been held in something more subtle: the weight of an order's continued grief, the specific gravity of an oath that had not yet been released. The Ebony Wake's vigil had been real devotion. Real devotion, sustained long enough, becomes its own kind of anchor. Caelen could not fully reach forward into the world while The Ebony Wake was still holding so tightly to the version of him that had fallen. The Forgotten Hero...wasn't actually forgotten. Lucian remembered him. After Kovinar, Vespertina, Squeaks, and Renzo had slain the last of the knights of The Ebony Wake, Lucian and Renzo were the only ones that remembered the so-called Forgotten Hero.

When Lucian surrendered the memory of his fall — not the love, not the history, just the specific night of the end...the reason for an oath to exist — the anchor lifted.

Caelen could reach.

Not yet. The clans were still fractured, the Hikarai still sealed, the man who would carry him not yet arrived at the Chamber. But the path was open in a way it had not been before. When Renzo Morokami finally stood before the statue and raised his sword in the same weary stance and the shadow moved across the floor and locked onto his — that was possible because of what Lucian had given away.

Lucian could not have united the clans. Varkhal had said so plainly: *not because you lack loyalty, not because you lack proof, but because you are not Japanese.* The thing Caelen's completion required was a resonance Lucian did not carry. His devotion was real but it was never going to be sufficient. He had been, all along, the preservation of a possibility he could not himself fulfill.

He did not know this. He knew only that something had changed — that a weight he had been carrying since before Kovinar was born had been transferred in a way he could not fully account for — and that finally allowed Caelen an opportunity to reunite the Pedronian Empire through a failed samurai named [[05_Player Characters/Renzo\|Renzo Morokami]].
