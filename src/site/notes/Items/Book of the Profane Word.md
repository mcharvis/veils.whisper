# Book of the Profane Word
**Wondrous Item, Artifact (requires attunement)**

> True evil is a festering thing.  
> It lies dormant in every person’s heart, its whisper a vile temptation toward a dark power.  
> That whisper is the Profane Word.  
> And bound within the pages of this twisted manuscript, that promised power is yours for the taking.

---

## Attuning to the Book
An **evil-aligned spellcaster** can attune by spending **80 hours** reading and studying its contents. Upon attunement:
- **Increase** one ability score by **+2** (to a **maximum of 24**).
- **Decrease** another ability score by **−2** (to a **minimum of 3**).

You can’t gain this effect more than once. The change **persists** even if you are no longer attuned.

---

## Unholy Being
**Attunement Shock.** When you become attuned to the Book of the Profane Word, you take **8d10 psychic damage**. The damage is absorbed by the spellcaster. Remaining damage will seek the lives equally within 120 feet.

**While attuned**, you gain the following properties:
- You have **resistance to necrotic damage**.
- Your **AC increases by 1**.
- **On a weapon hit**, the target takes an extra **1d6 necrotic** damage.
- **You can’t smell**.
- **All holy water** within **10 ft.** of you is **destroyed**.
- Your **weight drops by 1d4 × 5 pounds**.
- You have **disadvantage on attack rolls, ability checks, and saving throws** while **more than 30 ft.** from the Book of the Profane Word.


---

## Dark Whispers
While attuned, you can spend **1 hour** studying to **attempt to memorize** one of the **Spells of the Profane Word**.  
At the end of the hour, make an **Intelligence (Arcana)** check: **DC = 10 + (2 × spell level)**, then **gain 1 level of exhaustion**. On a success, you memorize the spell.

You can have a total number of **memorized spell levels** equal to your **character level** (e.g., at level 9: one 5th- and one 4th-level spell, or three 3rd-level spells).

- While a spell is memorized, you may **cast it using any slot** of appropriate level that you possess.  
- **Wizards & Sorcerers** may **copy** a memorized spell into their **spellbook**; once copied, it no longer counts against your memorized limit.
- If you **stop being attuned**, you **lose** any memorized spells.
