# III. The Binding Words of Vel'Amara

*Source: Recovered ritual text, Conclave of the Open Hand, Vel'Amara · fragment, origin unknown · two sections missing*

---

*This document was recovered from the Vel'Amara site during the second Ossuary Institute expedition, Year of the Greying 22. The expedition leader, Archivist-Field Tomas Renn, noted in his report that the document was found in a sealed stone box in what appeared to be a sub-basement of the Conclave's tower — a room that showed no signs of the catastrophe that had affected the rest of the building. The box was locked. The key was in the lock. Renn's report states that he found this detail "difficult to interpret as accidental."*

*Two sections of the text are missing — not damaged, but absent. The document was clearly formatted to include them: space was left, headers were written, but the sections themselves were not present in the box. Whether they were removed before the box was sealed, or whether they represent a ritual deliberately left incomplete, is unknown.*

---

**The Binding Words of Vel'Amara**
*A rite for the opening of a passage between the living world and the space of transit beyond the Veil*
*Composed by the Conclave of the Open Hand, for use in formal planar interface operations*

---

**Preamble**

These words are not a door. These words are the acknowledgment that a door exists and that we are requesting permission to stand at its threshold. The distinction is important. We are not opening anything. We are asking to be shown what is already open.

We address this acknowledgment to whoever manages the threshold — whose name we do not presume to know, whose title we do not presume to assign. We acknowledge only the existence of the management and the legitimacy of the request for permission.

If permission is not given, we will not proceed.

**Section I: The Announcement**

We are the living.
We stand at the near side.
We have studied the geometry of this place for twenty-three years.
We believe we understand the threshold.
We ask to be shown whether we are correct.

We bring no tools of extraction.
We bring no instruments of transit.
We bring only the instruments of measurement, which are the instruments of learning, which are the instruments of the Conclave's purpose.

We do not ask to cross.
We ask to look.

*[Section II: The Compact — text absent]*

**Section III: The Approach**

We open the following space, within the terms agreed:
Width of one arm's span.
Height of one standing person.
Duration of one turning of the glass.
Purpose: observation only, from this side.

We will not reach through.
We will not call to what lies beyond.
We will not record what we cannot see with unaugmented sight.

These are our terms.
We offer them in good faith.
We ask that good faith be returned.

*[Section IV: The Closing — text absent]*

**Section V: The Dissolution**

The space is closed.
The threshold returns to its prior condition.
We record what we have seen.
We submit it to the Conclave's archive.
We will not speak of it beyond these walls until the archive is complete.

We thank whoever managed the threshold.
We acknowledge that our access was granted, not taken.
We will not return without asking again.

*[End of text]*

## Vestl's Notes

The Binding Words of Vel'Amara are the most technically precise liturgical text in this collection and the one I find most terrifying.

They are terrifying because they are careful. They are terrifying because whoever wrote them understood exactly what they were doing and built every possible safeguard into the rite — the acknowledgment of management, the request rather than the demand, the strict terms of the opening, the explicit prohibition on reaching through or calling to what lies beyond.

They understood. They were cautious. They asked permission.

And something still went wrong.

The two missing sections — the Compact and the Closing — are what I think about at night. The Compact is where the terms of permission would have been received and agreed to. The Closing is where the transaction would have been formally ended. Both absent. Both deliberately absent, given that space and headers were prepared for them.

I can think of two reasons the Compact and Closing would be removed from a document before it was sealed in a box that was then locked and left in a sub-basement with the key in the lock.

The first: someone wanted the document preserved but did not want the terms of the Compact or the method of the Closing to be recoverable. They preserved the rite's structure but withheld its most sensitive content.

The second: the Compact was never completed, and the Closing was never performed. The rite was started. Permission was requested. Something in Section II went wrong — the Compact was not what the Conclave expected, or was offered on terms they could not accept, or was not offered at all — and the Closing was never reached because the rite never formally ended.

If the Closing was never performed, the space was never formally dissolved.

I do not know which reading is correct. I have spent considerable time trying to determine which one I hope is correct, and I find that I cannot answer that either.

The key was in the lock.

*— A. Vestl*
