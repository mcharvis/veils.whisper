# V. The Litany of the Nine

*Source: Recovered single manuscript, origin disputed · found inside the sealed tomb of an unidentified knight, Evergrowing Forest region, Year of the Greying 17*

---

*This text was recovered by an Ossuary Institute field team during a survey of ancient burial sites in the Evergrowing Forest region. The tomb in which it was found was sealed from the inside — a detail the field report notes without explanation, as the team could not determine how this had been accomplished.*

*The tomb contained nine sealed chambers. Eight were occupied. The ninth was empty.*

*The document was found in the ninth chamber, folded and placed on what appeared to be an altar of black stone, along with a single object: a gauntlet of blackened iron, empty.*

*The manuscript's origin is disputed. The scribal hand does not match any catalogued tradition. The language is archaic but legible. The Litany appears to be the ceremony through which the nine occupants of the tomb were bound — a binding they entered willingly, based on the text, for reasons the text implies but does not state.*

---

**The Litany of the Nine**
*A covenant rite for the binding of nine champions to a singular purpose and a singular master*

---

**The Caller speaks:**

We have called nine.
Nine have answered.
The ninth answers last, as is their place.

**The Nine respond:**

We answer. We are here.
We have come to the threshold knowing what waits beyond it.
We do not ask to be turned back.

**The Caller:**

Name what you are giving.

**The Nine:**

We give the remainder of our mortal lives, freely.
We give the service of our strength and judgment, freely.
We give the use of our names, which become the Caller's names to speak.
We give the right of waking, to be exercised at the Caller's need.
We do not give our deaths. Our deaths belong to the ledger.
Our deaths were written at our first breath.
We are giving what was already accounted for.

**The Caller:**

Name what you receive.

**The Nine:**

We receive purpose.
We receive the certainty of the ledger's recognition.
We receive the assurance that we will not scatter into the grey.
We receive waking, when waking is needed.
We receive rest, between wakings.
We do not receive freedom from the covenant.
We do not ask for it.

**The Caller:**

Is the covenant freely entered?

**The Nine:**

Freely entered.

**The Caller:**

Is the covenant entered in full knowledge of its terms?

**The Nine:**

In full knowledge.

**The Caller:**

Then the ledger receives the entry.
The names are written.
The covenant holds until the ledger burns.

*[The Nine are sealed.]*

*[The Caller addresses the ninth chamber.]*

**The Caller:**

The ninth place is kept.
Not empty — kept.
What is kept is waiting.
What is waiting will be filled when the need is greatest.
The ninth does not know yet what the ninth will be.

*[End of rite.]*

## Vestl's Notes

I am going to say what every reader who reaches this entry will be thinking and which scholarly convention would have me leave unsaid.

This is how it was done. This rite — or something close to it — is how the entity documented throughout Vol. I bound nine champions to its service: willingly, formally, with their explicit consent, in a covenant whose terms they understood and accepted.

The ninth chamber was empty. The ninth place is kept. What is kept is waiting.

The Knights of Nine — documented in various historical and contemporary accounts as agents of the Lord of Carrion, the Old King, the entity Serevane Doth spent five years arguing was Nerull — were not enslaved. They were not deceived, at least not about the terms of the covenant. They were told what they were giving and what they would receive, and they said: freely entered, in full knowledge.

I find this more disturbing than enslavement would be. Enslavement requires a villain and a victim. This requires only nine people who decided that purpose and the certainty of the ledger's recognition were worth the price.

*We do not give our deaths. Our deaths belong to the ledger.* They came to this covenant knowing they were already owned, in the theological framework they had grown up inside. They were not surrendering something they had. They were formalizing a pre-existing condition in exchange for something additional.

The Rite of the First Breath registered the claim. The Litany of the Nine fulfilled it in advance, with interest.

The ninth chamber was empty when we found the tomb. The manuscript does not say who fills it or when. It says the ninth does not know yet what the ninth will be.

It has been keeping me up at night. I include that information because Doth included his discomforts and it seems appropriate to continue the tradition.

*— A. Vestl*
