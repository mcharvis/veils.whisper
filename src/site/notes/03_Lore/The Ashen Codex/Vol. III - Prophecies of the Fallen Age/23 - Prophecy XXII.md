# Prophecy XXII
*Shard 18 · ms. BT-034 · second hand*

---

> The mother will eat her children
> the way winter eats a field —
> not with malice,
> but because that is what winter is for,
> and the field was always going to be eaten,
> and the mother was always going to be winter.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 44*

The third female figure. She is the darkest of them.

Not malice. That word is the hinge. The destruction here is not cruelty — it is nature. The mother does not harm her children out of hatred or ambition. She does it because that is what she is. The field does not deserve to be eaten; it simply is a field, in winter, and winter eats. The logic is not moral. It is categorical.

I have considered whether this entry and Prophecy II describe the same figure. The woman who brings you what was yours before you were born, and the woman who was always going to be winter — they may be one person at different stages of the same story, or two aspects of the same nature.

I do not know which reading is more disturbing. A single figure whose full character requires two texts to contain, or two separate figures of that magnitude operating in the same narrative.

The second hand on Shard 18 is the same calm hand as Shard 14. I am increasingly certain this is a single author, not a continuation scribe. Someone who knew what they were writing, and wrote it calmly, and went home afterward. I would very much like to know who that person was.

*— A. Senne*
