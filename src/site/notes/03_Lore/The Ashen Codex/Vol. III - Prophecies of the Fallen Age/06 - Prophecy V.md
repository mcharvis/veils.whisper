# Prophecy V
*Shard 4 · ms. BT-009*

---

> There was a king who fed his kingdom to a fire.
> The fire thanked him.
> The fire remembered him.
> The fire has not gone out.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 42*

The most historically grounded entry in the collection, in my estimation — and the one my colleagues have argued with me about most strenuously. Lorekeeper Aveth believes this is a direct reference to Rauconen of Elarion, the general who seized the throne after King Aldenor's death and whose reign coincided with the dissolution of that kingdom's ancient covenant. The fire, in her reading, is the accumulated suffering of those years. It thanks him because suffering accumulates; it remembers him because catastrophe has memory.

I find this reading too neat. Rauconen's fall postdates the accepted scribal period of the Testimonies by at least two centuries, and the phrasing — specifically *has not gone out* — is prospective in a way the historical reading cannot accommodate. A fire already extinguished cannot be described as ongoing. Either the scribal dating is wrong, or this is not Rauconen.

I leave it unresolved. My colleagues may be right. If they are, the dating problem is more serious than I have allowed.

*— A. Senne*
