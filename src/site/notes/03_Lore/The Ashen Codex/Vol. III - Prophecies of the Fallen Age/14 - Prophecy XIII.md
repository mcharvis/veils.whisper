# Prophecy XIII
*Shard 11 · ms. BT-021*

---

> She came to the old god with nothing and asked for everything.
> He gave it. He always gives it.
> He does not understand yet that giving is how he is emptied.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 43*

Shard 11 was recovered from the Nalyun archive during the second expedition, wrapped in cloth that the expedition's material analyst dated to within fifty years of the Fallen Age itself. Someone preserved this deliberately. Whatever they thought they were protecting, they wrapped it carefully and put it somewhere they believed would last.

The old god. The third time a divine figure appears in the Testimonies — the first two were unnamed; this one is gendered, aged, and characterized. He gives. He always gives. The repetition is not celebration; it reads as diagnosis. His generosity is his vulnerability. He does not see what the giving costs him because he has never experienced it as cost. It is his nature, and his nature is being used against him.

She came with nothing. This is precise. She is not powerful when she arrives. She has no leverage, no credentials, no threat to make. She asks. He gives. And in giving he is slowly reduced — not by force, not by conflict, but by the simple, patient mechanism of his own character.

This is among the most politically sophisticated entries in the collection. Whoever wrote it had watched power work at close range. They knew what it looked like from the inside of the court, not from outside it.

*— A. Senne*
