# Prophecy VIII
*Shard 7 · ms. BT-014 · partial; lower half water-damaged*

---

> The god does not see the student's other teacher.
> The student does not see the god's other student.
> [lacuna — approx. two lines lost]
> When the lesson ends, one of them will have learned nothing.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 42*

The water damage is frustrating. Whatever the lost two lines contained, they sat between the problem and the conclusion — between the establishment of the double-blindness and the declaration of its outcome. The ending tells us what happens. The missing lines may have told us why.

The structural logic here is unusually clear for the Testimonies. Two parties, each operating with incomplete information about the other's situation. The asymmetry is not power but knowledge. And knowledge, the prophecy implies, is what the lesson was for. One party completes the lesson having gained nothing from it.

This is a betrayal text. I am fairly certain. Someone is being used as an instrument of education by a teacher who does not know the student has another teacher. The student learns from both. The god learns from neither. When the lesson ends, the god has been outrun by the thing it was trying to train.

I find this the most psychologically precise entry in the collection. Whoever wrote it understood power and its limits with a specificity that does not read as allegorical. It reads as personal.

*— A. Senne*
