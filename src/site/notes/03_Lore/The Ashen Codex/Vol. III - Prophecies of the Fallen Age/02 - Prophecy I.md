# Prophecy I
*Shard 1 · ms. BT-003*

---

> The first door opens inward.
> The second opens only once.
> The third has no handle on either side.
> What passes through it does not know it has passed.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 41*

The oldest entry in the collection by scribal analysis. The hand is clean, unhurried — whoever wrote this was not transcribing from memory or dictation but composing in place. That is unusual for the Testimonies, most of which show the small errors and corrections of someone racing against time or firelight.

Three doors. The image recurs in death-cult literature of the period, though never in this precise formulation. Other texts describe two gates, a river crossing, a weighing of hearts. The three-door schema is distinct. It implies process — not passage but stages of passage. The third door is the troubling one. No handle on either side suggests neither volition nor return. The passage through it is unconscious.

I read this as liturgy, not prophecy. A description of death as the Fallen Age understood it, stylized into three acts. The lack of subject — no pronoun, no named traveler — makes it universalizing rather than specific. This is not about a particular death. It is about what death is.

I note it here because the image of the third door recurs twice more in this volume, in entries I am not yet prepared to call liturgy.

*— A. Senne*
