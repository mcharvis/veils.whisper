# Prophecy XX
*Shard 17 · ms. BT-031*

---

> He has wanted to die for so long
> that dying has become a kind of home.
> When the living finally give him what he wants,
> he will thank them.
> He will mean it.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 43*

The only entry in the Testimonies that I would describe as tender. Death here is not passage, not transformation, not threshold. It is relief. The figure who receives it has been waiting for it long enough that waiting has become structural to his existence. Dying is home because home is where you have been long enough to stop noticing the walls.

He will thank them. He will mean it. The emphasis on sincerity is unusual. Most gratitude in death-cult literature is formal, liturgical — the correct response at the correct moment. This is personal. This is a man who genuinely, in the moment of his death, is grateful to the people who ended him.

There is something in this entry that the others do not have. I am not sure what to call it. Grief, perhaps, for a figure I do not know and cannot identify. I find him more real than most of the other presences in this collection, and I do not know why.

*— A. Senne*
