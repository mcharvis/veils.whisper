# Prophecy XXIII
*Shard 19 · ms. BT-036*

---

> At the end there is a door.
> It opens inward.
> You have been here before.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 44*

The final entry in the recovered corpus. Three lines — the same length as Prophecy I, which also concerned a door. That door opened inward. This one does too. The collection closes where it began, with the same image and the same direction. I do not know if this is intentional, coincidental, or a consequence of how I have ordered the shards.

*You have been here before.* Second person, the only instance in the entire collection. Every other entry is third person — she, he, they, the wolves. This one addresses the reader directly. You. Not a figure in the text. The person holding it.

I have tried to find a way to read this as liturgical, as conventional, as something I can explain. I have not succeeded. The door opens inward. You have been here before. The collection ends and the ending is a recognition — not of an arrival but of a return.

I do not know what that means. I do not know if I am meant to find it comforting. I find that I cannot say.

*— A. Senne*
