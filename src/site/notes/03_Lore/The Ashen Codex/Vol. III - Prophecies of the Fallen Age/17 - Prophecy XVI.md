# Prophecy XVI
*Shard 14 · ms. BT-026*

---

> What passes through the Veil does not return unchanged.
> What does not pass through the Veil does not return at all.
> The unchanged and the unreturned
> are harder to tell apart than you would expect.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 43*

The Veil appears by name here for the first time in the Testimonies. It is not the only instance — it recurs in entries XVIII and XXIII — but it is the first, and the framing is definitional. The Veil is a threshold that changes those who cross it. This aligns with the death-theology of the period: passage into the next world was understood as transformation, not preservation. The self that arrived was not the self that departed.

The second line is the interesting one. *What does not pass through the Veil does not return at all.* This implies a path that requires the crossing — that attempting to return without having crossed it simply fails. Not prevented; absent. The path is not blocked; it does not exist.

The final two lines are, I think, darkly comic — which is unusual for the Testimonies. The changed and the gone look alike. You expect to be able to tell. You cannot. Whatever the Veil does to those who cross it, the doing is subtle enough to deceive the living.

I have been asked, at lectures, whether this entry frightens me. I have declined to answer. I decline here as well.

*— A. Senne*
