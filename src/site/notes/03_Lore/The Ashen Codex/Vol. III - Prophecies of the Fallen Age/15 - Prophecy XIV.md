# Prophecy XIV
*Shard 12 · ms. BT-022*

---

> Wolves are herded into the coming of winter.
> Prepared to die, they carry keys of bone and a blessing for the hand that leads.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 43*

This entry is sourced from a single fragmentary manuscript recovered from the lower archive at Vis'Avae, designated Shard 12. Its provenance is disputed. The script matches the hand of the Fallen Age scribes catalogued by Maren of Duskhollow, though several colleagues have raised objections I consider unpersuasive.

The imagery here is conventional for the period: wolves as a metaphor for the condemned, winter as the inevitable terminus of mortal ambition. Keys of bone recur in at least four other Testimonies — see entries IX, XI, and XXII — and appear to represent formal rites of burial passage, the dead carrying credentials for whatever waits. The hand that leads is almost certainly a reference to a death-priest or psychopomp figure, possibly specific to a cult that predates the formalized church.

What I find most arresting about this entry is not its imagery but its assumption. It does not announce catastrophe. It treats catastrophe as already decided. The wolves are not warned. They are prepared. Whatever hand leads them has already been identified, already accepted. The text was written after the choosing, not before.

I have been unable to identify a historical event that satisfies this reading. That is either because no such event has yet occurred, or because I am looking in the wrong century.

I suspect the latter.

*— A. Senne*
