## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 44*

I include this entry despite its condition because of what can be read. *The crown does not make the queen. The queen was made before the crown.* Whatever else is lost, that sequence survives, and it is enough to change the meaning of every other coronation-adjacent entry in this collection.

If the crown is not constitutive — if it does not make the person who wears it — then it is something else. Confirmation, perhaps. Recognition. The formalization of something already true. And if the queen was made before the crown existed, then the crown's arrival is not a beginning. It is a conclusion.

I have written in the margin of my working copy: *who made her, and when, and for what.* I do not have answers. The four lost lines may have provided them. They are gone.

I am more troubled by this partial entry than by several of the complete ones. What is missing from an incomplete text has a way of filling itself in with whatever the reader most fears.

*— A. Senne*
