# Prophecy XI
*Shard 9 · ms. BT-018 · second hand*

---

> The bone under the earth is not a bone.
> It is a word in a language the earth has forgotten.
> When it is spoken aloud, the earth will remember.
> What the earth remembers, the dead will hear.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 42*

The second hand on Shard 9 is the most agitated of all the secondary scribes. The pressure is uneven. Several letters are started and restarted. Whoever took over the writing after the first hand stopped was in some distress.

Bone as language. The dead hear when it is spoken. This is ritual terminology — specifically, the terminology of exhumation rites, in which objects buried with the dead were sometimes removed and used as instruments of communication with those same dead. The key-of-bone image in Prophecy XIV recurs here, though this entry predates it in the shard sequence. They are speaking to the same practice.

*What the earth remembers, the dead will hear.* This implies the earth has heard things the dead have not — that burial is not disappearance but deposit. The dead persist in the ground the way words persist in a language no one speaks. They require retrieval, not resurrection.

I find the image of a language the earth has forgotten more disturbing than any of the explicit death-imagery in this collection. Knowledge that no living thing holds. Knowledge that must be disinterred.

*— A. Senne*
