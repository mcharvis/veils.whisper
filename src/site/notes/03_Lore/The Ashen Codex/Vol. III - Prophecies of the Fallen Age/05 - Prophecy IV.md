# Prophecy IV
*Shard 3 · ms. BT-007*

---

> The crow does not mourn.
> The crow does not wait.
> The crow lands on the highest branch
> and counts what the field has given it.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 42*

Recovered from the lower archive at Vis'Avae, the same site as several other shards in this collection. That so many fragments survived the city's various catastrophes is either remarkable luck or evidence of deliberate concealment. I incline toward the latter.

The crow is the single most consistent image in the Testimonies. It appears in eleven of the nineteen recovered shards, in varying roles — observer, messenger, inheritor, judge. Here it is a counter. It surveys aftermath. The field has already given; the crow is taking stock.

I read this as a figure for patience — specifically the patience of something that does not operate on human timescales. The crow does not mourn because mourning requires investment in outcome. It does not wait because waiting implies that time is passing against its will. It simply persists until conditions are favorable and then counts what is owed.

This is either a god or a philosophy. I have not decided which is more troubling.

*— A. Senne*
