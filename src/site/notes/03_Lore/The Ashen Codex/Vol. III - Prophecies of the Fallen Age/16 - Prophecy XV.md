# Prophecy XV
*Shard 13 · ms. BT-024*

---

> The man who raised the wolf
> will stand at the wolf's grave
> and not know whether to call it mercy.
> He will decide.
> The decision will cost him everything after.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 43*

The wolves continue. Three entries running now — XIV, and this one — and the image has shifted from collective to singular. One wolf. One man who raised it. The intimacy of the formulation is new. This is not a metaphor for armies or condemned peoples. This is two individuals and a grave between them.

The man raised the wolf. He was responsible for it — literally, in the sense of care and formation, not merely observation. The wolf's death is established before the entry begins. The man is already standing at the grave when we arrive. What is uncertain is interpretation: was this mercy or was it something else?

He will decide. Not *he has decided* — he will. The grammar implies the decision is still ahead of him, though the event it concerns is already past. And the decision will cost him everything after. Not before. After. Whatever he chooses, he will carry it forward. The weight is prospective.

I find the compassion in this entry striking. Whoever wrote it was not interested in condemnation. They were interested in the man who has to live past the moment. That is a rare concern for death-cult literature of this period. Most of these texts end at the threshold. This one asks what happens to those who don't cross it.

*— A. Senne*
