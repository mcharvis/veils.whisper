# Editor's Closing Note

*Aldric Senne, Lorekeeper Third Class, The Ossuary Institute*
*Year of the Greying 44*

---

Three years have passed since the preface. I will not pretend the work is complete. Four shards remain unlocated. One is so damaged as to be nearly useless. The cosmological material — the fragments I designated for Vol. IV — remains unsorted on my desk.

I am less certain than I was when I began. I had expected the opposite. Scholarship usually produces certainty as a byproduct, even when it does not produce answers. You learn the shape of the problem. You learn what can and cannot be known. That shape becomes something you can hold. I do not hold this one. I carry it.

My colleagues at the Institute have begun to use language I find imprecise. They speak of the Testimonies as living documents — as texts that remain active in some sense, that continue to accrue relevance as history unfolds. Lorekeeper Aveth has suggested, in print, that the Fallen Age prophets had access to knowledge that exceeds natural explanation. I have not responded to this publicly. I am preparing a response. It is taking longer than I expected to find the right register for it, which is to say I am finding it difficult to disagree with her as completely as I believe I should.

Vol. IV will address the nature of Nerull's domain as the Fallen Age understood it, the records of what they believed waited at the end of the third door. I have begun reading those fragments. I will say only that they are consistent with the prophetic material in ways I had not anticipated, and that consistency is either evidence of a coherent underlying source or evidence of something else I am not yet prepared to name.

The Ossuary Institute has served this work well. I am grateful to my colleagues, to the expedition teams, to the archivist at Vis'Avae who kept the lower vaults through circumstances I understand were not easy. Whatever these texts are, they survived because people decided they were worth surviving. I find that, at the end of three years, to be the thing I am most certain of.

I expect to be at this desk for some time yet.

*— A. Senne*
*The Ossuary Institute, Duskhollow*
*Year of the Greying 44*

---

*The Ossuary Institute dissolved seven years after this publication.*
*Vol. IV was never written.*
