# Prophecy IX
*Shard 8 · ms. BT-015*

---

> She who carries the key does not know it is a key.
> She who carries the key does not know she is carrying it.
> The door it opens was made for her before the key was made.
> Before she was made.

---

## Notes on Source and Attribution
*Aldric Senne · Year of the Greying 42*

Another female subject, the second in the collection. She connects, in my reading, to the figure in Prophecy II — the woman who brings something that was yours before you were born. Here the inversion: she carries something she does not know she is carrying, toward a purpose that was designed for her before she existed.

The key-and-door image is the most common in all of death-cult literature. I have documented forty-seven variations across the corpus. What distinguishes this entry is the temporal sequence — door, then key, then bearer — and the implication that none of the three are accidents. The door was not waiting for any key. It was waiting for this one. And this key was not made for any lock. It was made for her.

I have, in weaker moments, found this entry upsetting. I include that only because I believe intellectual honesty requires it. I do not know what it is about this text that produces that response. I have read it perhaps a hundred times. The feeling does not diminish.

*— A. Senne*
