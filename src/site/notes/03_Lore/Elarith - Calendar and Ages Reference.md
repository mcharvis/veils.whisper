## The Problem of Time in Elarith

Elarith does not have one calendar. It has several, each belonging to a different era, none of them perfectly reconcilable with the others. This is intentional — civilizations that collapse take their calendars with them, and the people who rebuild afterward start counting from something they understand.

What follows is the working framework established for *The Veil's Whisper* and the Ashen Codex. Treat the numbers as scholarly consensus, not divine certainty. Doth himself notes that the Fallen Age's terminus is disputed depending on which event you select.

---

## The Four Eras

### Era 0 — Before the Counting
*No calendar. Deep time.*

The period before any surviving record. Nerull exists here. The Litany of the Nine was likely performed here. The covenants that shaped everything that followed were made in this era by entities that did not need to count years because they did not expect to run out of them.

Nothing from this period is recoverable in any conventional archival sense. It surfaces only in the oldest liturgical fragments — the ones that predate even the Fallen Age's scribal tradition — and in the behavior of gods who remember it.

**What lives here:** Nerull's original accumulation. The first binding of the Knights of Nine. 

---

### Era I — The Fallen Age
*Calendar: Years of the Fallen Age (FA) · Approx. FA 1–1,200*

The Fallen Age was a period of high civilization — the most administratively, magically, and theologically sophisticated era in Elarith's recorded history. It lasted approximately twelve centuries before collapsing, not from invasion or catastrophe, but from managed dissolution.

The calendar counted forward from a founding event no longer recoverable. The Ossian civil service used it. The Pale Cartographers used it. It is the most precisely documented calendar in the Ashen Codex.

**Key dates:**

| Year (FA) | Event |
|---|---|
| Approx. FA 760 | The Sundering of Vel'Amara — the Conclave of the Open Hand opens a gate. The city falls silent in three days. |
| Approx. FA 800 | The Accord of Dravan's Mouth — the Grey Emissary brokers a coalition treaty. Six powers sign. |
| FA 803 | The Three Thrones of Ossian — formal covenant begins with the Lord of the Third Account. |
| FA 803–864 | The Rite of the First Breath mandatory in Ossian. Every newborn registered in Nerull's ledger. |
| FA 864 | The Three Thrones covenant completes. Third throne vacated. Ossian begins its quiet decline. |
| FA 940 | The Pale Cartographers begin operations. |
| Approx. FA 1,070 | Survey Mark 7 documented — the Veil is absent, not merely thinned. The absence is maintained at a fixed size. |
| FA 1,076–1,087 | The Unmaking of the Silver Road — eleven years of compound marginal deterioration. |
| FA 1,087 | Last waystation on the Silver Road closes. |
| FA 1,091 | The Pale Cartographers dissolve. Final resolution: *what happens next is not our work to do.* |
| Approx. FA 1,091 | The Cartographer's Last Entry — an unnamed member returns to Survey Mark 7 one final time. |
| FA 1,099 | The Last Conclave of Duskhollow — thirty-one scholars identify the managed dissolution. Torvel Ash names the agent plainly. He disappears six weeks later. |
| FA 1,101 | The Ossuary Institute founded by Lorekeeper Maren. |
| Approx. FA 1,200 | **The Collapse** — the Fallen Age ends. Not dramatically. It has been ending for a century. The last functioning institutions cease to function. |

**The Fallen Age ended approximately 3,000–4,000 years before the current campaign.**

### Era II — The Long Interval
*No calendar. The dark between.*

Three to four thousand years of rebuilding without memory. The world does not forget the Fallen Age so much as it loses the ability to remember it. Languages shift. Borders dissolve. The archive Maren built in FA 1,101 passes through hands that gradually understand it less and less.

Nerull is quiet during this period. Not absent — consolidating. The grey between stabilizes at the levels his cultivation produced. Survey Mark 7 remains open. The bones of the Fallen Age sink into the ground and become archaeology.

Xaphan is imprisoned during this interval. He has been waiting for most of it.

**What lives here:** The slow transformation of the Ossuary Institute from Maren's archive into something she would not recognize. The long patience at Survey Mark 7. Xaphan in the grey, counting nothing.

---

### Era III — The Years of the Greying
*Calendar: Years of the Greying (YG) · Current era*

The current calendar. It counts from the founding of a new civic order in Duskhollow — the specific event is a matter of local historical debate, but the calendar stuck because the Ossuary Institute used it and the Institute's records were the most comprehensive surviving documentation of anything.

The name "the Greying" refers to the quality of the age that preceded it — the long dim interval between the Fallen Age's collapse and the current world's reconstruction. The scholars who named it were looking backward at something they could barely see.

**Key dates:**

| Year (YG) | Event |
|---|---|
| YG 1–10 | Ossuary Institute archive recovery begins. First expeditions to Vis'Avae. |
| YG 11 | The Cartographer's Last Entry found in the Duskhollow archive — inside a sealed leather case bearing the Pale Line guild mark. |
| YG 14 | Silver Road trade records compiled by the Duskhollow Institute of Commerce. |
| YG 22 | The Binding Words of Vel'Amara recovered from a sealed sub-basement. The key is in the lock. |
| YG 28–33 | Serevane Doth compiles Vol. I — *Histories of the Fallen Age.* |
| YG 33 | Doth writes the Prayer of the Third Door. Hides it inside the Pale Cartographers' survey report. |
| YG 34–39 | Auren Vestl compiles Vol. II — *Liturgies of the Fallen Age.* |
| YG 36 | Serevane Doth dies at his desk, mid-sentence, working on Vol. IV. |
| YG 39 | Fire at the Vis'Avae archive. Shard 5 of the Blackened Testimonies destroyed. |
| YG 41–44 | Aldric Senne compiles Vol. III — *Prophecies of the Fallen Age.* |
| YG 44 | Vol. III published. Last honest work the Ossuary Institute produces. |
| YG 51 | The Ossuary Institute dissolves and reforms as a death cult. Vol. IV never written — by Doth. |

## Where the Campaign Sits

The current campaign — *The Veil's Whisper* — takes place in the **Years of the Greying**, some time after YG 51 (the Ossuary Institute's dissolution). The exact current year in the Greying calendar has not been formally established.

**What we know about the current moment:**
- Xaphan has been imprisoned for approximately **1,600 years** — confirmed by Session 00's title *"Sixteen Centuries Late."* His imprisonment began during the Long Interval, roughly 1,600 years before the present.
- The Fallen Age ended **3,000–4,000 years** before the present.
- The Years of the Greying began after the Long Interval. If the Fallen Age ended ~3,500 years ago, and Xaphan has been imprisoned for 1,600 years, his imprisonment began roughly 1,900 years after the Fallen Age's collapse — midway through the Long Interval.
- The Ossuary Institute's publications (YG 33–44) are in living institutional memory — the cult that replaced it still exists. The Greying calendar is therefore not thousands of years old. It likely counts in the low hundreds at most. A reasonable working assumption: **the current year is approximately YG 300–500**, though this has not been formally established and should be set intentionally when it matters.

## The Seasons of Elarith

The Elarith year is divided into six seasons of roughly sixty days each. The calendar was not designed by scholars — it was felt out by people who noticed that the world had moods, and that the moods had rhythm, and that the rhythm tracked the Veil.

The Veil thins in cold. It holds in warmth. The seasons are a map of that thinning.

| # | Season | Quality | Veil Condition | Real-World Equivalent |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| 1 | **The Waking** | First thaw. The dead stir at the edges. | Thinning from winter | Late Feb – Apr |
| 2 | **The Green** | Full spring. Life loud. Growth everywhere. | Thickening | May – Jun |
| 3 | **The Long Light** | Midsummer. Heat. The world at its most alive. | Thick — grey quiet | Jul – Aug |
| 4 | **The Dimming** | Days shorten. Something in the air shifts. | Beginning to thin | Sep – Oct |
| 5 | **The Grey** | Deep autumn. Cold arriving. The world holding its breath. | Thin | Nov – Dec |
| 6 | **The Long Dark** | Full winter. The Veil at its thinnest. The grey loudest. | Thinnest | Jan – Feb |

The current campaign sits in **The Grey**, moving toward **The Long Dark.** The party can feel it — shorter days, cold coming, the sense that the world is tilting toward something.

The prophecy — *wolves are herded into the coming of winter* — places the endgame in **The Long Dark.** That is not accidental.

## Days of the Week

Seven days, named after forces already present in Elarith's lore. Not invented — derived.

| Day | Named For | Character |
|---|---|---|
| **Ashday** | The ash of the Fallen Age | First day. Somber start. |
| **Crowday** | The crow — patience, observation | Market day in most settlements |
| **Veilday** | The Veil itself | A day for rites, burial, remembrance |
| **Ironday** | Labor, craft, the material world | Work day |
| **Thornday** | The wilds, danger, the road | Travel day |
| **Greyward** | The grey between — the threshold | Uneasy. Many won't begin journeys or sign contracts. |
| **Stillday** | Rest. Silence. End of the week. | Contested holy day — see below |

**Greyward** sits between the working week and the rest day — the threshold between. Superstitious people avoid important decisions on Greyward. The Ossuary Institute death cult observes it as their most sacred day. Most common folk just call it unlucky.

**Stillday** has a quiet theological arc. It was Nerull's day — observed in silence and stillness, the correct posture for property awaiting collection. The Raven Queen's faithful are reclaiming it as a day of passage rather than accumulation. The theological contest between the two observances is ongoing and occasionally violent in settlements where both factions have presence.

## Sacred Observances

### The Night of the Long Rest
*Midpoint of The Long Dark — the longest night of the year*

Nerull's holy day. Observed under the old order in total silence and stillness from sundown to sunrise — the correct posture of the owned dead, performed annually by the living as rehearsal.

Under the new order it is contested. The Raven Queen's faithful observe it as a night of passage — fires lit, names spoken aloud for those who crossed in the preceding year, the six words said clearly into the dark. The death cult in Duskhollow still sings the Canticle of the Kept from sundown to sunrise.

The most charged night in the calendar. If something significant is going to happen, it happens on the Night of the Long Rest.

---

### The Greying
*Midpoint of The Grey — deep autumn, the threshold before winter*
*Real-world: mid-to-late November*

A sacred observance of the Raven Queen's faithful. Not a holy day in the institutional sense — it predates any institution. It is the moment when the Veil's thinning becomes palpable, when the world is clearly moving toward the Long Dark and there is no pretending otherwise.

The faithful gather. They light fires against the cold. They speak the names of those lost in the preceding year. They read from *The Book of the Unburied* — whatever passages they have memorized or managed to preserve, since physical copies are rare and hunted. They say the six words together.

*Go through. Don't stop in the grey. The grey is not your place.*

The Greying is not a celebration. It is an acknowledgment. The world is thinning. Winter is coming. The wolves are being herded. The faithful choose to face that together rather than alone.

> *In the world of Elarith, the Greying is the night the faithful of the Raven Queen gather at the threshold of the Long Dark and remind each other what they are walking toward — and why it is worth walking toward it.*
