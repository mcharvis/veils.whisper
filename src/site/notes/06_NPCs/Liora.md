**Status:** Active — Oasis Civil Assembly
**Role:** Council member. Reluctant spymaster. The only honest politician in a city that doesn't reward honesty.
**Affiliation:** [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Oasis\|Oasis]] Civil Assembly

---

Liora Vane was not born into power. Her father dealt in lumber from [[The Evergrowing Forest\|The Evergrowing Forest]]. Her mother worked as a healer. They were neither poor nor comfortable — the kind of family that pays its debts on time and watches its neighbors pay the price for other people's carelessness.

She grew up understanding, before she had words for it, that politics was not about speeches. It was about survival. Who got protected. Who got left exposed. Who controlled the language used to describe both.

Her early reputation was built in the lumber guild disputes — a young woman with no family name behind her, quick-witted and unafraid of confrontation, fiercely protective of common citizens who had no one else willing to sit across a table from men twice her age and say *no*. She won her Assembly seat on a promise that sounded radical only because no one in Oasis had bothered to say it aloud before: that both noble houses and councilors would be held accountable. She meant it. That was the dangerous part.

She has spent every year since proving she meant it, and making enemies accordingly.

---

Liora is blunt in a city that rewards obliquity. She speaks in short, precise sentences. She does not perform warmth she doesn't feel, and she does not soften information to protect the feelings of people who should know better. Where [[06_NPCs/Councilor Varn Drest\|Councilor Varn Drest]] twists words like knives, Liora uses hers like nails — hammered in until no one in the room can ignore them.

She walks the poorer districts of Oasis without guards. She listens directly to complaints and files them into Assembly disputes even when it wins her nothing. She has a strong base among the guilds, the healers, and the traders — the people the noble houses have always treated as scenery — and she has held that base by being consistent in a place where consistency is rare enough to be remarkable.

She distrusts nobility on principle. Not out of resentment, or not only — she has watched too many good-faith offers from noble houses turn out to be traps, and the habit of skepticism has calcified into something she does not always examine carefully enough. She sometimes dismisses things that deserve consideration. She knows this about herself and considers it an acceptable cost.

What she values above everything else is evidence. Not testimony. Not reputation. Evidence. If you bring her proof, she will act on it regardless of what it costs her. If you bring her only words, she will be polite and she will not move.

---

The party first encountered Liora in the aftermath of [[06_NPCs/Councilor Varn Drest\|Councilor Varn Drest]]'s corruption becoming impossible to ignore. She had been working against him for years — isolated in the Assembly, more respected than supported, aware that Drest was dangerous and lacking the leverage to prove it publicly.

She came to them by lanternlight in the forest outside [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Jade Stronghold\|Jade Stronghold]], pressing a forged writ of entry into their hands and making the stakes plain: *"We have about three days. Expose her imprisonment. Bring evidence. Drest cannot be allowed to bury this."*

She did not promise them safety. She did not promise them gratitude. She gave them a tool, told them what needed doing, and trusted them to do it — which was, for Liora Vane, as close to warmth as she gets.

When they delivered, she held her end. She coordinated intelligence through [[06_NPCs/Kaelin Vetch\|Kaelin Vetch]], kept political pressure on Drest through [[06_NPCs/Captain Rel Thorn\|Captain Rel Thorn]] and [[06_NPCs/Velexia\|Velexia]], and worked to ensure that what the party had done could not be quietly erased by the people with the most to gain from silence.

Her position since the siege of [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Jade Stronghold\|Jade Stronghold]] is complicated. Drest escaped. [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier John Halric\|Sanctifier John Halric]] — whose location she was tracking — was dead before she could use that information. Her tiefling spy sent to Ahrown has not reported back. She is crippled in terms of active intelligence and knows it.

*"Kovinar, have you any sign of Drest? We can't seem to locate him. We sent a tiefling spy to Ahrown... but she hasn't reported back. We'd check the VE but it's full of psychopaths and liars — so he'd blend right in. We're stuck on locating him, but he's crippled politically in Oasis."*

She is not defeated. She is recalibrating.

She wants Oasis stable. She wants Drest exposed and removed. She wants the Assembly returned to something resembling accountability — not justice, not justice is too much to ask for in a city like this, but accountability. The small, grinding, unglamorous kind where the people responsible for decisions have to live with the consequences of them.

She has no interest in being a hero. She does not think about legacy. She thinks about the city she grew up in, the one her father came home to smelling of sawdust and her mother left before dawn to serve, and she has decided — quietly, without ceremony, in the way people decide things they intend to keep — that it is worth fighting for. Indefinitely. At whatever cost the fighting requires.

The party is, at present, the most effective tool she has. She knows that. She is not sentimental about it, and she would not insult them by pretending otherwise. If they become a liability she will say so plainly, to their faces, without cruelty and without hesitation. And when that day comes — if it comes — she will still be someone worth having stood beside.
