*Knight of the Forgotten Tomb · Oath-Brother of [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]]*

**Status:** Deceased. Fell in the clearing during the search for the Crown of the Quiet Sovereign, protecting [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]].

## The Vigil

Sir Lucian Caelmoor was one of four knights who stood vigil within the [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Temple of the Forgotten Hero\|Temple of the Forgotten Hero]] — stationed in deliberate formation, sworn to protect a memory. Their armor was worn, their blades nicked with age. Their discipline remained absolute.

They did not speak. They did not move unless the tomb was violated. And when they did, it was with terrifying unity.

Lucian was once the most outspoken of the [[04_Organizations/Ebony Wake\|Ebony Wake]], a knightly order devoted to ideals over kings. He followed [[06_NPCs/Caelen, Firstborn of the Flame\|Caelen, Firstborn of the Flame]] into the final campaign and stood beside him through [[the final betrayal\|the final betrayal]]. When the Hero fell, Lucian did not seek vengeance. He and three others chose a different oath, one of remembrance through service, not bloodshed.

He bound himself to the tomb willingly. Not through necromancy. Not through divine coercion. Through the specific, voluntary stubbornness of a man who had watched someone give up everything including his name, and decided the least he could do was make sure the place he rested was not left unguarded.

---

## The Name He Kept

When Caelen commanded his erasure, every member of the Ebony Wake obeyed. His name was removed from records. His deeds were struck from the order's histories. Every knight honored the command the way soldiers honor orders they disagree with: completely, and at great personal cost.

All but one could bring himself to full compliance.

Lucian, his family name lost to the same erasure he had helped enforce, could not erase Caelen from himself. He had tried. He had struck the name from every document he controlled, had spoken it aloud for the last time on the night they sealed the tomb, had walked away from [[02 World/The Pedronian Empire\|The Pedronian Empire]] and taken up his vigil at the [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Temple of the Forgotten Hero\|Temple of the Forgotten Hero]] in the [[The Evergrowing Forest\|Evergrowing Forest]], where there was nothing but stone and memory and the specific grief of a man who had watched someone choose to become forgotten.

But he could not let go of the name entirely.

So he did the only thing that couldn't be ordered away. He *became* it.

He took *Caelen* as the root of his own surname, not Caelen itself, which would have violated the spirit of the command even if not its letter. Instead: *Caelmoor*. Caelen's moor. The keeper of the empty ground where a great man once stood.

A moor is a landscape of stripped things, wind-scoured, rain-flattened, bare. It holds nothing except the memory of what grew there before the weather took it. Lucian named himself after the landscape of grief. After the thing that remains when someone great is gone and you are the only one left who knows exactly what the place looked like before.

He guarded the Temple for centuries. Not because Caelen was there. Caelen was never there. The Temple is a monument the way the Tomb of the Unknown Soldier is a monument: honorable, mournful, and empty of the man himself. Lucian guarded it because someone had to, and because standing in an empty place with a name carved into your own identity is sometimes the only form of loyalty available.

## The Battle and the Salvation

Three of the four knights were killed when [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]], [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]], [[05_Player Characters/Squeaks\|Squeaks]], and [[05_Player Characters/Renzo\|Renzo]] entered the Temple. The knights engaged because the party had not yet demonstrated what they were. The battle was the test. It was always the test.

Lucian was the last standing when Kovinar crossed the chamber with his weapon lowered. He placed a hand on Lucian's shoulder and said:

*"By the grace of Bahamut, may your soul find its true path."*

The [[Items/Magical/The Cursed Cross\|Cursed Cross]] flared gold. The chamber went still. Lucian knelt.

Then he stood. He picked up his sword. He followed Kovinar out of the Temple and into the world, because a man who invokes divine grace for the sake of an enemy's soul is the kind of man a knight of the Ebony Wake recognizes. Not as Caelen. But as someone worth following for what Caelen's reasons were.

The name *Caelmoor* went with him. Still carrying what it had always carried.

---

## The Years With Kovinar

Lucian traveled with Kovinar as an oath-brother for years, long enough that the men who had known him at the Temple would not have recognized the way he laughed now, or the patience he showed people who reminded him of himself at twenty. He swore a new oath to [[06_NPCs/Gods/Bahamut\|Bahamut]], through Kovinar, without abandoning what the old oath had meant.

He did not speak of Caelen often. He did not need to. The name was in his surname. The grief was in the set of his shoulders, and slowly, over the years, something gentler had grown in beside it. He had found, very late and almost by accident, people worth being unguarded around. Kovinar most of all.

He never got around to telling him that out loud. He ran out of time before he ran out of nerve.

## The Clearing

He found the end of it in a clearing he had never seen before, fighting for a man who had been twisted into something wearing his face and none of his will. Lucian did not hesitate at the sight of it. He had spent four hundred years guarding an empty room for someone he loved who could never thank him for it. He understood loyalty that asked for nothing back better than almost anyone alive. He had simply never expected it would be the thing that finally killed him.

The first blade found him before anyone could call out a warning, and then a second, and then a third, and by the time he understood he was dying he had already decided what to do about it. He went down on one knee the way a man kneels when he still intends to stand. Something in his chest, the oath he had carried since before most of the people in that clearing were born, refused the verdict. For one breath, maybe two, he was alive on nothing but stubbornness, and like every other breath he had spent in four hundred years, he spent it on someone other than himself.

The Black Wolf, ten feet away and powerless to close the distance, stopped his own fight entirely to watch him rise. He had raised men who could do that once. He knew exactly what it cost, and what kind of man chooses to spend it a second time.

It bought Lucian one more breath. Varian, or the cold thing using Varian's hands, did not offer him a chance to spend a third. The cut that found him the moment he stood back up was the only one that mattered. The ones that followed were excess, were certainty dressed up as cruelty, were a thing that had stopped recognizing him as a person making absolutely sure he stayed that way.

Lucian went down kneeling. Not thrown. Not broken. Kneeling, the way he had spent four centuries kneeling before an empty tomb, except this time there was no standing back up, and the last thing his eyes found before they stopped finding anything was Kovinar's face.

He did not say anything. There was nothing left to spend on words.

Something in Kovinar that had been holding very still since the first sword was drawn stopped holding still. He does not remember choosing to fight differently after that. He remembers wanting Varian dead with a clarity that frightened him more than the battle did. For one unbearable moment, the boy underneath surfaced, eyes clear, hand finding Kovinar's arm in the chaos like a man with one sentence left and no time to spend it. *"She's not... listen... she's not who..."* Then the dark closed over him again, harder than before, and what was left turned on Kovinar with nothing held back.

Kovinar did not let it cost him a second person that night.

He ended it. Cleanly, finally, the way a man puts down something that is already gone and only cruel to keep walking. There was no triumph in it, and he never once mistook it for any. Killing the thing wearing his friend's face did not give him Lucian back. It only meant someone paid for what had happened, and there was no one else left in the clearing to make the payment.

The fire did not come back after that. The clearing kept the rest of it the way clearings keep most things, quietly, without explanation, except for one man who had also lost something that night and recognized the shape of it on someone else's face. The Black Wolf knelt beside the boy he had raised and said nothing for a long while. When he finally rose, he did not look at Varian again. He looked at Kovinar.

He pulled a plain iron ring from a cord around his neck, the only thing on him not built for killing, and held it out without quite letting go of it yet.

*"You put him down when I couldn't,"* he said. *"That's not nothing."*

Then he said the thing he meant for both the dead, though only one name belonged to it.

*"He was better than what I made him. I never told him."*

Kovinar said nothing back. There was a ring in his hand and a body at his feet that had not been a body an hour before, and between the two of them there was no room left that night for anything but carrying both.
