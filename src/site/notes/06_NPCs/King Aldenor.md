## The King He Was

Aldenor was a good king. That is the tragedy of him — not that he was weak or cruel or foolish, but that he was none of those things and it still wasn't enough.

He ruled [[Elarion\|Elarion]] with the kind of steadiness that courts take for granted until it's gone. He tried to cut [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|his brother's]] power with laws rather than blades, believing that justice could outpace treachery. He was wrong about that. By the time he saw Rauconen's hand on the knife, it was already too late for cleverness.

So he made a bargain. He went to [[06_NPCs/Gods/Nerull\|Nerull]] and offered his soul — not immediately, but stretched across a thousand years, long enough to see his daughter grown and his kingdom safe. He believed he had been clever. He believed he had bought time.

Nerull honored the bargain in the only way he knows: completely, and without mercy.

The night Rauconen's assassins came for [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]], their blades passed through her like mist. Nerull pulled them into Aldenor's chest instead. He woke long enough to understand what had been done to him — that he had been outwitted by a god, that the bargain had been kept to the letter and violated in spirit, that his daughter was alive and he was not.

Then he died. And his soul was Nerull's to claim.

## The King He Became

Elarith does not break men cleanly. It stretches them — pulls at the edges of who they were until the shape holds but the substance is gone. Aldenor found his way to [[Ahrown, Kingdom of Elves\|Ahrown, Kingdom of Elves]], a dead city in a dead land, and discovered that the crown still fit. He rules it now as the Mad King: a corpse in court regalia, his robe bleeding sap like a dying tree, his crown fused to his skull, one eye blind and glowing faintly blue — he claims it sees the past as it was meant to be.

He is not entirely wrong. He remembers Vespertina with perfect clarity. Everything else — the crown, the court, his own name at times — fades and shifts and blurs. But her face, her laugh, her first steps, the way she clung to her mother's gown: those are carved into him. They are the reason, he says, that he has not gone fully mad.

The madness is evident anyway. He switches tones mid-sentence. He whispers conspiracies to himself. He touches the faces of strangers, seeking texture clues to confirm their identity. He asks the party what year it is, then scoffs when they answer — *"That's not the real year."* He keeps a room full of clocks, none of them ticking.

He refers to his sword as his second daughter, Vespertina. He calls the wind his mother's voice. He is not performing. He genuinely does not know where the edges of reality lie anymore.

Nerull did not break him all at once. He just gave him nothing but memory, stretched across centuries, until memory became a blade that cuts instead of comforts.

## What He Did in Ahrown

He took the throne by treachery. He does not pretend otherwise.

[[06_NPCs/Queen Vaeloria, The Hollow\|Queen Vaeloria, The Hollow]] wore the crown before him — ruled Ahrown before it fell, before Aldenor arrived and found a kingdom of the dead waiting to be claimed. He struck her down and cast her into [[01 Campaigns/Encounters/2 - Ahrown/Encounter 2.5 - The Bottomless Pit\|the Bottomless Pit]], an endless wound in the earth beneath the city. She survived, as the dead tend to in Elarith, and clung to the Black Sigil — a necromantic artifact that had bound the throne's power.

When the party arrived, Aldenor sent them into the Pit to retrieve it.

What they found there was not simply a rogue necromancer. Vaeloria was the true queen of Ahrown, and she told them so — her voice moving through the absolute dark like something with no body, no breath, no mercy. *"He calls me monster,"* she said. *"But what do you call a man who steals what was never his?"*

Vespertina destroyed the crimson phylactery bound to the back of Vaeloria's skull. The voice screamed and vanished. The crown fell.

When they returned, Aldenor did not flinch from what he had done. *"I took her throne,"* he said plainly. *"She held the crown before me. It was by treachery that I wear it now. Do not mistake me — I am king of Ahrown, and you are its princess."*

## The Revelation

He recognized Vespertina before she recognized him...but he initially thought that it was madness.

It surfaced slowly — the clothes on her back, the fire in her eyes, something in the line of her jaw that no curse could fully erase. He circled it, approaching and retreating, speaking of dead children and bargains with gods, until finally he could not hold it back any longer.

*"Behold and gaze upon my face, Princess Vespertina Galanodel. Do you not recognize the face of your father?"*

He told her everything. The bargain. Rauconen's knives. The way Nerull had honored the letter of his promise while gutting the spirit of it. He spoke of her mother, [[06_NPCs/Sorceress Aurienne\|Aurienne]], and that he had not stopped loving either of them in all the centuries since.

*"You are the only thing I remember clearly,"* he said. *"Everything else fades — the crown, the court, even my own name at times — but you, Vespertina, you are carved into me."*

He lifted the curse that had masked her heritage. Gold light burst from his crown. For the first time, she could see her own face as it was meant to be.

Then he gave her the [[Items/Book of the Profane Word\|Book of the Profane Word]] — one of Nerull's spellbooks, bound in black flesh — and charged her with something more dangerous still.

## The Harbinger

The sealed crate in the throne room looked empty to everyone in the party.

Except Vespertina.

Only she could see the gleam of steel within it, the faint shimmer of a blade that did not want to be seen. Aldenor's hands trembled against the crate as he spoke of it — not with greed or ambition, but with something closer to fear.

*"This is the Harbinger Blade. In their hands, it is the end of all things. Every company I have sent to carry it has returned broken, hollow-eyed."*

He told her to take it south, beyond the merchants and the liars and the scars of war, to the [[02 World/The First World Tree\|First World Tree]] — older than Nerull's whispers, older than death itself. If anything could swallow the blade and keep it from the [[04_Organizations/The Horsemen\|Four Horsemen]] closing in on Ahrown, it was there.

*"Good luck, my child. This will not be easy. Your friends may betray you. If you fail, it will not be only our crown that rots — it will be all that is."*
