## Overview

Valamaradace is gold-scaled and old enough that scholars argue over whether she predates the Fallen Age or merely survived it. She does not speak where silence will do the work instead. Where Bahamut judges and weighs, Valamaradace simply holds — wards, thresholds, the things that need a guardian rather than a champion.

## Myth & Origins

Elarion was not the first city Valamaradace loved. Long before it, in the high-rising spires of Everlund, the Mother-City whose towers once touched the stars, she walked among elves as Valara Eryvell, hiding her draconic nature beneath silver scales and a calm, lilting voice, teaching what she called the way of mercy and flame. There she loved a mortal elf-lord, Odran Eryvell, and in that love broke a law older than either of them: a gold dragon does not bear mortal kin. Discovery meant two different reckonings. Bahamut's order would have stripped her of the council seat and everything she'd earned among her own kind. [[06_NPCs/Gods/Corellon Larethian\|Corellon]]'s clergy, had they known whose blood now ran in one of their own chosen lines, would have called it a desecration of the Seldarine itself, and elves do not forgive that kind of insult quietly. She kept it from both of them the same way she kept everything else, and the daughter she bore carried her spark forward, a mercy fierce enough, it would turn out, to defy death itself.

When Everlund fell, to planar shadow and to a war neither side fully understood, Odran died defending it. Valamaradace could not save him without revealing what she was, and either god learning the truth would have cost her daughter everything. She let him die rather than risk it, and has never spoken of the choice to anyone, then or since.

She took their daughter, Lyriel, and what refugees Everlund had left, and led them to found the settlement that would, in time, become [[The Elven Empire of Elarion\|Elarion]]. Lyriel's line continued in turn, a daughter, Elanaura, and her daughter after her, [[06_NPCs/Sorceress Aurienne\|Aurienne]]. Ages passed. Valamaradace went on concealing what she was, the way she always had, until at last she withdrew from mortal sight entirely.

Gold dragons keep their own order among themselves, a hierarchy older than most mortal kingdoms, and at the top of it sits a council of seven, ancient wyrms who attend to Bahamut's will directly rather than through priesthood or proclamation. A seat on that council is not given. It is earned by surviving a trial Bahamut sets personally, one that few gold dragons are ever offered and fewer still survive with their judgment intact. Valamaradace earned hers so long ago that even the other six no longer agree on what her trial was.

What is agreed on is what she did with the centuries afterward. Most of Bahamut's seven take their turns walking the world in human or elven guise, the way gold dragons have always preferred to watch over what they're sworn to protect, close enough to intervene, distant enough not to be asked to. Valamaradace took to this more thoroughly than her kin. She did not drift from court to court. She kept the same bloodline she'd already given everything to protect, mother to daughter, generation past generation, long after everyone who might have asked her why had stopped existing to ask.

Long before any of that, before the council seat and the centuries of careful watching, [[Elarion\|Elarion]] knew her the way it knows its oldest trees. She still works alongside what's left of it: elven rangers who've never seen her true shape and wouldn't believe it if they had, druids who leave offerings at certain trees without knowing why their grandmothers taught them to, the scattered remnants of elven kingdoms older than Elarion itself. Her lair sits somewhere inside that forest's natural and magical borders, woven into them rather than carved out of them. No one has mapped it. No one who's tried has come back able to describe where they'd been.

She does not hoard gold. Other dragons mistake this for poverty, which amuses her more than it should. What she keeps instead are libraries: histories no living scholar remembers writing, tomes in scripts that predate the Fallen Age, the kind of patient, accumulated knowledge that takes centuries to gather and seconds to lose. She reads. She has always read. It is the one appetite age has never dulled in her.

And once, before [[06_NPCs/Lord Rauconen\|Rauconen's]] knives and [[06_NPCs/Gods/Nerull\|Nerull]]'s quiet bargain, she was bonded to the protectors and magic-users of [[The Elven Empire of Elarion\|Elarion]] at its brightest, the way her kind has always gravitated toward civilized places that still believe magic and harmony belong in the same sentence. She did not stop that fall. Whatever she did or didn't do when it came, she has never spoken of it, and no one has been foolish enough to ask twice. What's left of her devotion to that kingdom narrows now to the one line still worth keeping.

She is rarely seen and almost never heard. When something in that lineage is threatened badly enough to warrant her attention, the sign of her passing is not roar or fire. It is a spiral, burned clean into stone, left behind the way a name is left on a grave.

Valamaradace is the matriarch of the draconic bloodline carried through the elven mothers of [[The Elven Empire of Elarion\|Elarion]]'s royal line, daughter to daughter from Lyriel to Elanaura to [[06_NPCs/Sorceress Aurienne\|Aurienne]], and through her, [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]]. Valamaradace is keeper of that lineage as much as keeper of any realm, and is inclined to protect it quietly, without credit, the way she does most things. This is the origin of Vespertina's Gold Dragon ancestry. Valamaradace's mark is a spiral burned into stone, left wherever she has intervened. One such mark was found in the marble where Aurienne vanished.
