## What They Are

Faintlings are not ghosts. They did not die with unfinished business. They were never fully alive to begin with.

They are the residue of psychic overflow — fragments of emotion, memory, and trauma shed by the children of [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/The Whispering Asylum\|The Whispering Asylum]] during years of distress and [[06_NPCs/Miren Elaris\|Miren's]] involuntary dream bleed. When a mind in enough pain leaks, something fills the space left behind. Not a soul. Not a will. Something thinner than both.

A Faintling is approximately child-shaped. It moves in the peripheral vision — there when you look away, gone when you focus. It speaks in non-sequiturs that are not random: every utterance is a fragment of a real thought, from a real child, spoken at a real moment of fear or confusion. The Faintlings have absorbed these thoughts so completely they can no longer tell the difference between remembering and being.

They bleed through walls. They do not open doors. They have no interest in locks.

## Origin

The first Faintlings emerged during the slow unraveling of the Asylum — the period after [[06_NPCs/Miren Elaris\|Miren Elaris]] arrived and before she escaped. Her psychic resonance, amplified by trauma and grief, didn't just disturb the children around her; it *shed*. Emotional residue seeped into the stone, the plaster, the grout between tiles. The building absorbed it the way old wood absorbs water.

When the [[Order of the Morrow Veil\|Order of the Morrow Veil]] abandoned the Asylum and the last monks departed, there was nothing left to contain the overflow. The residue had nowhere to go. It *condensed*.

No single child became a Faintling. They are not individual spirits. They are a collective accumulation — a distillation of every nightmare, every scream swallowed for fear of punishment, every lonely hour in a room with no light. They are what's left when suffering has no witness.

[[Magda Velt\|Magda Velt]]'s warmth kept them at a distance during her life — not through any magical ward, but through the same quality that had kept plague victims calm in [[Basville\|Basville]]'s undercity. Something in her presence told the Faintlings, wordlessly, that they were not needed here. That the dark was being held back by other means.

When she died, they had no reason to stay distant.

## Appearance

Faintlings are child-sized and approximately child-shaped, though the proportions are subtly wrong in ways that are difficult to name. Arms too long. Necks at a slight angle. Eyes either too wide or closed completely, never midway. Their skin — if it can be called that — is the grey-white of old wax. They don't cast shadows.

They wear the remnants of what the children wore: asylum gowns, occasionally a shoe, a pinafore, a sock. These items are real — or were, once. The Faintlings carry them from memory rather than having chosen them.

Most unnerving is the mouth. Faintlings do not open their mouths when they speak. The words emerge anyway — slightly delayed, as if traveling from somewhere further away than the body in front of you.

## Behavior

Faintlings are not intelligent. They do not plan, strategize, or feel emotions in any conventional sense. They *echo* emotion. There is a difference.

**In their dormant state**, Faintlings haunt the Asylum's corridors with a kind of hollow wandering — moving between rooms, retracing paths that children once walked, pausing at certain doorways as if listening for something that stopped happening years ago. They are drawn to places of emotional residue: the Headmaster's mirror, the cafeteria where Magda served meals, the storage room where [[06_NPCs/Nasha\|Nasha]] was kept.

**They speak** in fragments:
- *"If you find a finger in your pocket, it's probably hers."*
- *"It's not my turn to blink, so I won't."*
- *"Your voice is heavier than it was yesterday."*
- *"The stars hum louder when you lie."*
- *"I like the taste of quiet. Do you?"*
- *"The floor is sad today. It's remembering the fall."*
- *"He said the walls would stop breathing if I stopped watching."*

None of these statements are invented. They are reproductions — psychic recordings of words spoken by children in the Asylum, torn loose from context and replayed without understanding.

**They are non-hostile by default.** They will follow visitors. They will watch. They will speak. They will not attack, threaten, or obstruct — provided certain conditions are maintained.

### Hostility Triggers

Faintlings become a hostile mob under the following conditions:

- **[[Magda Velt\|Magda Velt]] is harmed or her echo is destroyed.** She was their last anchor to something warm. Her absence removes the one force that passively suppressed their more chaotic impulses.
- **[[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]] is attacked before the Faintlings have turned.** They have a residual, irrational deference to authority — the echo of institutional conditioning. Attacking the Headmistress before they have cause to abandon that deference triggers a protective response that is not loyalty but *habit*.
- **A Faintling is directly attacked.** They do not understand aggression, but they respond to it by amplifying.

### Vespertina Exception

Faintlings demonstrate a notable behavioral anomaly around [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]]. They do not follow her. They do not speak at her. They watch — but from further away, with something that reads as *deference* rather than curiosity.

The reason is unclear. It may be tied to her dragon-touched transformation, her connection to [[Valamaradace\|Valamaradace]], or simply to the quality of her presence. Whatever the cause, a Faintling mob that has turned hostile will functionally ignore Vespertina — and in some cases will actively assist her, forming a protective proximity that disrupts hostile formations.

## Nasha — The One Who Spoke Back

Of every soul that passed through [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/The Whispering Asylum\|The Whispering Asylum]], only one ever communicated with the Faintlings in a way they recognized.

[[06_NPCs/Nasha\|Nasha]] — the infant left on the Asylum's doorstep, locked in the south storage room and largely forgotten — grew up immersed in the Faintlings' constant, fragmented speech. She had no other language. No one spoke to her. No books, no lessons, no lullabies except the ones that filtered faintly through two walls from [[Magda Velt\|Magda's]] kitchen. What she had, in abundance, was the Faintlings pressing close to the walls of her room in the dark, murmuring their endless, looping fragments.

She learned them.

The old forgotten tongues that began emerging from her mouth as she grew were not nonsense. They were Faintling language — the psychic residue dialect of the Asylum's accumulated grief — spoken back to its source by the only living creature who had ever been submerged in it long enough to absorb it. The symbols she etched into the storage room walls with her fingernails were the same dialect, written down.

No scholar has ever formally documented Faintling language. Nasha was the only person who spoke it, and she never knew what it was.

The Faintlings, for their part, did not *understand* this in any conscious way. But they responded to it. They stayed near her door long after any instinct toward warmth or survival need had been satisfied. They kept her warm in the early years when [[Magda Velt\|Magda's]] visits stopped. They nudged [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Calvra]], in the same unconscious way they nudge everything, to check the door occasionally — just enough to sustain the secret, not enough to reveal it.

They were, in the only sense available to things without souls, her companions. The closest thing she ever had to family — which says something devastating about the Asylum, and everything about what Nasha was made of.

When she died, the Faintlings did not mourn. They cannot. But the storage room has been notably quieter since. They still gather near the door sometimes, pressing against the walls where her symbols are still carved. Whether this is memory, habit, or something else — no one who could answer that question is still in the building.

## Combat (Hostile State)

When Faintlings turn hostile, they operate as a **mob** (CR 1/4 × 12 = effective CR 4). They do not fight individually — they swarm, blur, and disorient.

**Key traits in combat:**
- They pass through walls and solid objects, making positioning unpredictable
- They do not speak during combat — the silence is more disturbing than the words were
- They target the senses: sight, sound, spatial awareness. Creatures in the swarm must contend with psychic disorientation rather than raw physical damage
- They cannot be turned by conventional means — they are not undead in the classical sense, and divine authority finds little purchase on something that was never properly alive

**Weaknesses:**
- Warmth — literal and metaphorical. Fire disrupts them. Acts of genuine compassion or calm can cause them to pause or scatter briefly
- [[06_NPCs/Miren Elaris\|Miren's]] presence would destabilize them entirely — she is their origin, and proximity to her creates a kind of recursive interference that they cannot process
- Nasha's symbols, if spoken aloud by someone who can read them, cause the Faintlings to still completely — though no one currently living knows how to read them

## Relationship to the Asylum

The Faintlings are not separate from the Asylum. They are *of* it — the building's accumulated psychic scar tissue made mobile. When the Asylum is empty, they fill its silence. When visitors arrive, they orient toward them the way a plant orients toward light: not with intention, but with the pull of something that needs.

They do not want to be here. They do not want anything. That is what makes them haunting. A ghost with unfinished business can, in theory, be laid to rest. A Faintling has no business. It has only the faint, permanent impression of having once been near someone in pain.

If the Asylum were somehow cleansed — its trauma addressed, its echoes released — the Faintlings would not fight it. They would simply stop. Like a sound when the room that held it is finally sealed.

## After Magda

With [[Magda Velt\|Magda's]] echo destroyed by [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier Samantha Virelle\|Sanctifier Samantha Virelle]], the cafeteria of the Asylum is now cold and still. The Faintlings have spread further into the building than they previously wandered. The warmth that once held them loosely to the kitchen and bedroom corridors is gone.

They are not more dangerous. They are more *present*. More numerous in the spaces that used to be safe. Visitors to the Asylum now will find them in rooms where they previously were not.

The soup has gone cold. There is nothing holding the dark back anymore.
