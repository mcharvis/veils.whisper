**Role**: Military Commander of Thornhold  
**Personality**: Loyal, ruthless, pragmatic  

> "You bring me results, not excuses. If you're here to waste my time, the gate's behind you."

**Backstory**:  
A war orphan raised in Thornhold's barracks, Rel owes everything to the Vaer-Lorne name. He sees the Sanctifiers as a threat worth any cost to eliminate.

---

He doesn't remember his parents' faces. He remembers, with total clarity, what was left of the village they died in, and the Sanctifier banner someone found half-burned in the wreckage, the only piece of evidence anyone ever offered him for why. The Vaer-Lornes took in the orphans the raid left behind because it cost them nothing and looked good in the histories. Rel was six. He grew up sleeping in Thornhold's barracks instead of its nursery, fed on soldier's rations instead of a noble child's table, and somewhere in that gap between what he was given and what the family's own children received, he decided gratitude and resentment could occupy the same chest without either one winning. He stayed anyway. The Keep was the only home that had ever bothered to be one, even at a remove.

He earned his rank the unglamorous way, decades of competence rather than a single dramatic victory, and the Countess trusts him for exactly the reason she trusts almost no one else in Thornhold: he has never once asked her for anything beyond his post. Where the rest of her court postures and angles, Rel simply does the job in front of him and reports plainly when it's done badly. She doesn't know, or has decided not to know, how much of his loyalty to her family is really loyalty to the memory of a six-year-old who was chosen instead of left.

His hatred of the Sanctifiers isn't policy. It's personal, old, and entirely uninterested in nuance. He doesn't care that Oasis's councilors find them useful, or that men like [[06_NPCs/Councilor Varn Drest\|Drest]] have quietly decided their protection is worth the coin. To Rel, every banner that organization flies is the same one he found in the ash as a child, and he has never once pretended otherwise in front of anyone who outranks him, which has made him exactly as many enemies in Oasis's polite circles as you'd expect.

He runs Thornhold's garrison the way he was raised: results over excuses, competence over breeding. Soldiers who came up the way he did get a longer leash from him than nobles' sons who bought their commissions. He isn't cruel about it. He simply doesn't pretend the second kind has earned anything yet.
