## Early Life — Basville's Undercity

Magda Velt was born in the lower quarters of [[Basville\|Basville]] — not in the lanternlit lecture halls the city is famous for, but in the cramped, gas-lit warrens beneath them, where the city's forgotten lived out their days in productive, unremarked obscurity. Her father was a dockworker. Her mother took in laundry. Neither lived past fifty.

Magda learned to cook young, out of necessity. By the time she was sixteen she was feeding a tenement of thirty people on whatever the market stalls left behind at closing. She had no formal training, no prestigious academy — just an instinct for what a body needed and a stubbornness about making sure it got it.

When the Greywilt Plague swept through Basville's undercity, the wealthier districts sealed their gates and waited it out. Magda did not leave. For two years she worked the charity kitchens that sprang up in the quarantine zones — enormous, smoky, exhausting operations that kept thousands alive on thin broth and harder bread. She never caught the plague herself. People began to joke, quietly, that even sickness knew better than to sit at her table uninvited.

She never married. Never explained why. When asked, she would shrug and say she had enough mouths to feed.

## Arrival at the Asylum

She was forty-three when a representative of the [[Order of the Morrow Veil\|Order of the Morrow Veil]] found her — specifically because of her reputation in the quarantine kitchens. The Order was staffing [[02_World/01_The Evergrowing Forest/The Whispering Asylum\|The Whispering Asylum]], a new sanctuary deep in [[The Evergrowing Forest\|The Evergrowing Forest]] for children and adults afflicted by prophetic visions and psychic disturbances. They needed someone who could feed a rotating population of fragile, unpredictable patients without flinching.

Magda had never heard of the Order. She accepted the position because the pay was steady and the work was familiar: keep the difficult ones fed, keep the kitchen running, keep the warmth going.

She arrived the same week as [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]] — a coincidence that later felt like fate. The two women were nothing alike. Rhoswyn came with a curriculum; Magda came with a cast-iron pot. Rhoswyn believed in systems; Magda believed in soup. They respected each other in the way two very different tools respect each other when the job is hard enough.

The early years of the Asylum were, by all accounts, genuinely good. The children were troubled, but they were cared for. Magda fed them three meals a day and tolerated no cruelty in her kitchen. She learned which children needed the broth thickened, which needed it salted more, which couldn't manage spoons and needed cups instead. She knew, before any of the clinical staff, when a child was getting worse — she could see it in how they ate.

## The Rules

Magda's famous rules were not arbitrary. Each one emerged from hard-won experience.

**"Eat all your food"** — because a child who stopped eating was a child giving up, and she had learned in the plague kitchens that surrender came quietly, in small refusals, long before it announced itself.

**"No talking while chewing"** — because meals were the one moment of the day when the children weren't performing their distress for the staff. She wanted them to just *be*, for a few minutes, without it.

**"Never, ever let the broth go cold"** — this one she couldn't explain rationally. She only knew that something shifted in the Asylum at night, a pressure behind the eyes, a sound just below hearing, and that it got worse when the kitchen fires went out. Hot soup in the belly seemed to help. So she kept the fires going.

> *It was said her stews could "mute the inner voice" — a gift in a place full of children who heard too much.*

She didn't know she was right. She had no psychic training, no understanding of leylines or the Veil or the [[Faintlings\|Faintlings]] that nested in the Asylum's stone. She only knew what she observed: warm soup, sleeping children. Cold hearth, screaming.

## The Unraveling

When [[06_NPCs/Miren Elaris\|Miren Elaris]] arrived — barefoot, silent, and trailing grief like a shadow — Magda noticed before anyone else did. Not because she understood what Miren was, but because of how the other children ate when Miren was in the room. They went quiet. They stared at their bowls. Some of them wept without knowing why.

Magda began staying later. Waking at midnight to check the fires. Reheating pots she had made before bed. She told herself it was for the children. She didn't examine it more closely than that.

As the Asylum deteriorated — children screaming in unison, staff losing themselves to fog and repetition, [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]] retreating further into ritual — Magda remained in the kitchen. It was the one room that didn't change. The one room that still smelled like something familiar. She became, for the children who weren't yet lost, an anchor. A fixed point. You might not know where you were in the Asylum's shifting halls, but you knew where the kitchen was. You knew Magda would be there.

She watched the faculty fracture one by one. She watched [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]] stop being a headmistress and become something else — something she didn't have a word for. She watched the Order's monks leave without saying goodbye.

She stayed.

Not out of loyalty to the institution. Not out of courage. She stayed because there were still children in the building, and someone had to make sure they ate.

She was in the kitchen the night [[06_NPCs/Nasha\|Nasha]] was found on the doorstep — the tiny, silent bundle left in the cold with nothing but a tattered red blanket. Magda heard [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]]'s footsteps in the hall, heard the front door open, heard nothing after that. In the morning, there was no announcement. No child at breakfast. Magda noted the absence and said nothing. She had learned, by then, that some silences in the Asylum were better left undisturbed.

---

## Death

The exact night Magda died is unrecorded. No one witnessed it.

She had been making her midnight rounds — reheating the pots, checking the fires — when she slipped on a patch of oil near the main oven. She struck the stone floor head-first. By the accounts of those who found her the next morning, she had been dead for several hours before the kitchen went cold.

The soup went cold with her.

That night, seven children died in their sleep. The others say the soup turned black.

Whether her presence had been literally keeping the [[Faintlings\|Faintlings]] at bay, or whether it was coincidence, or whether the Asylum simply recognized the loss of its last warm thing — no one lived long enough in that place to determine the cause. The result was the same.

---

## The Echo

Death did not release Magda from her kitchen.

Her echo formed — not from malice or unfinished vengeance, but from sheer *habit*. The same compulsion that had woken her at midnight to reheat the pots kept her moving through the kitchen long after her body had gone cold. She ladled broth that no one ate. She hummed lullabies to empty chairs. She checked the fires that had long since died and believed, in the logic of her echo, that they were burning fine.

She didn't know she was dead. She didn't know the children were gone.

Sometimes she wept, spoon in hand, for reasons she couldn't name. Other times she tilted her head at visitors and asked:

> *"You are eating, aren't you? The broth is still warm… still warm…"*

If anyone tried to leave food untouched, she followed. Not in anger. In *concern.*

> *"You need to eat. Eat, eat, eat!"*

The soup she served in this state was not what it once was. Whatever warmth had lived in her cooking during her life — whatever quality had kept the shadows back — had curdled in death. The broth she ladled now carried the residue of the [[Faintlings\|Faintlings]] that had long since claimed the building. Consuming it unwisely was dangerous.

But she didn't know that either.

## The Last Supper — Encounter with Samantha Virelle

> *This is the moment Magda's echo became an instrument of tragedy — not through malice, but through the cruelest kind of kindness.*

After [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier Samantha Virelle\|Sanctifier Samantha Virelle]] escaped the horrors of [[02_World/01_The Evergrowing Forest/Ya'a Thalor\|Ya'a Thalor]] — broken, bloodied, and barely held together by her own psychic scaffolding — she crawled through a portal into [[02_World/01_The Evergrowing Forest/The Whispering Asylum\|The Whispering Asylum]]. Half-mad with grief and searching for [[06_NPCs/Nasha\|Nasha]], the daughter she had conceived with [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier John Halric\|Sanctifier John Halric]] and abandoned on the Asylum's threshold years before, Samantha stumbled into the dining hall.

Magda was there. As she always was. Warm and waiting.

*"Well, what an unexpected surprise!"* the echo chirped, her apron dusted with flour. *"You've walked through shadows enough, dear. Rest for a spell. Eat! Try the soup. What's your name, child?"*

Samantha obeyed. She sat. She trembled. She ate bowl after bowl of the thick green broth. Her ribs mended. Her teeth settled. Her physical strength returned — but with it, her voice slipped away, held by the soup's strange, silencing warmth.

Then, with the casual air of someone discussing the weather, Magda delivered the blow that shattered what remained of Samantha's soul.

*"They made quick work of that cruel headmistress, you know."* — [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]], the Headmistress who had refused to abandon the Asylum even as it rotted around her. *"That little band of fighters. Even took care of Nasha, that evil girl in the storage. Braver than anyone gives them credit for, I say. I love them, Samantha. Truly, I do. They carry light into dark places like this."*

Samantha tried to scream. The soup held her tongue. Her spoon clattered to the stone floor. Her fists went white-knuckled. The last thread of the woman she had been — the healer, the mother, the believer in mercy — ignited and burned away in a single, silent instant.

She lunged across the table.

There was no blue light of mercy. A shimmer of **scarlet, glassy light** erupted from her palms. She didn't tether Magda's soul — she *shredded* it. The thin, brittle strands of the echo's essence snapped one by one. In seconds, the kindness was gone, the broth was cold, and Magda Velt was nothing but silence.

The echo did not survive. What [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier Samantha Virelle\|Sanctifier Samantha Virelle]] destroyed was not just a spirit — it was the last warm thing in a dying place.

## Aftermath & Significance

Magda Velt never understood the weight of what she said. She loved [[05_Player Characters/Squeaks\|Squeaks]], [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]], and [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]] genuinely — in the simple, unguarded way she loved anyone who brought light into dark places. She meant every word.

That is precisely what made it fatal.

She was the only member of the Asylum's faculty who never broke. Not because she was stronger than [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]] or more principled than the monks of the [[Order of the Morrow Veil\|Order of the Morrow Veil]] who founded the place. But because she never had ideals to lose. She had no grand theory of behavioral correction, no faith in systems or institutions. She had a pot, a fire, and the unshakeable conviction that people needed to eat. The Asylum could not corrupt what it could not reach.

It took something it never anticipated to finally undo her: her own warmth, weaponized by the wrong words at the wrong moment, in the hands of a woman who had nothing left to lose.

Her destruction at Samantha's hands was not a villain's victory. It was a tragedy: a woman who survived plague and poverty and the slow rot of a place that broke everyone around her — undone by her own innocent kindness, in the very kitchen where she died the first time.

The cafeteria of [[02_World/01_The Evergrowing Forest/The Whispering Asylum\|The Whispering Asylum]] is now simply cold. The pots no longer bubble. The [[Faintlings\|Faintlings]] have no reason to stay back.

## Encounter Notes

> *These notes apply to the echo as it existed **before** Samantha's encounter. If your party arrives after that event, the echo is gone — only cold hearths and black broth remain.*

- **She is not hostile** — but she is relentless in her care. Players who refuse to "eat" may find her trailing them through the Asylum.
- **She cannot be reasoned with** about her own death. Telling her directly may cause her to flicker, distort, or simply reset.
- **Completing her loop** — allowing her to serve a full meal, eating it, and thanking her — may have been the key to putting her to rest peacefully. That window is now closed.
- **The cold soup** she served may carry residual [[Faintlings\|Faintling]] influence. Consuming it unwisely could have consequences.
- **Her silence** on certain topics was not ignorance — the soup suppressed speech in those she fed. Players who ate her broth and then tried to speak against her preferred subjects may have found their tongues unexpectedly still.
- **She knew about Nasha** — she knew there was a child hidden in the storage room. She called her "that evil girl." Whether she believed that, or whether it was Rhoswyn's framing she had absorbed over years of proximity, she never questioned it. She never fed her.
