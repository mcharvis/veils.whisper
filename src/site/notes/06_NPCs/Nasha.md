## Overview

Nasha was a child who was never supposed to exist, in a place that was never supposed to keep her, growing into something that no one ever tried to understand.

She was the secret daughter of [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier Samantha Virelle\|Sanctifier Samantha Virelle]] and [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier John Halric\|Sanctifier John Halric]] — a union that violated every code of the [[04_Organizations/The Sanctifiers\|Sanctifier Order]], a pregnancy that was commanded to be terminated, a birth that happened anyway in a cold storage room beneath [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Ya'a Thalor\|Ya'a Thalor]] while her father lectured junior acolytes on spiritual purity one floor above.

She spent her entire life in a single room. She died in it.

She was a teenager when the party found her. She looked younger.

---

## Birth — The Candle and the Crate

Samantha Virelle carried Nasha for seven months in secret — defying [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier John Halric\|Halric's]] direct command to terminate the pregnancy, hiding her body beneath Sanctifier robes, learning to breathe around the weight of what she was keeping.

She gave birth alone, in a sub-level storage room beneath [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Ya'a Thalor\|Ya'a Thalor]], biting down on her own leather sleeve to muffle the pain. No midwife. No witness. Just a single lantern swinging above her, casting stuttering shadows across the blood-slick floor.

The child was small. Quiet. Alive.

Virelle held her for exactly one minute. She wrapped her in a strip of red ceremonial cloth torn from a discarded Sanctifier tablecloth — still reeking of ash and incense — and wiped the blood from the girl's eyes with trembling fingers.

> *"I'm sorry, Nasha. You were never supposed to be."*

She walked through the forest for hours. She placed the bundle on the stone threshold of [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/The Whispering Asylum\|The Whispering Asylum]]. She kissed the girl's forehead once.

She never looked back.

The name *Nasha* was never spoken aloud to the child. It was only ever whispered by her mother in that storage room, in the dark, before she left. Nasha never knew she had it.

## Discovery — Rhoswyn Calvra's Mercy

It was [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]] who found her — woken by a silent scream in her sleep, following the sound not with her ears but with her mind. When she opened the Asylum's front door, she did not flinch at the infant left to the frost.

She examined the girl's eyes — silver-gray and wide — and recognized the Sanctifier sigils sewn into the hem of the red cloth. She knew exactly what this was. A scandal. A liability. A secret someone had made flesh.

She could have turned the child away. She could have sent word to [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier John Halric\|Halric]]. She did neither.

> *"You do not belong here,"* she whispered.

Then she took her inside and locked her away.

She cleared out the south storeroom — cold, no windows, no mirrors — and put the infant there. She told the staff and the children that a remote chapter had taken in a foundling. She wrote it in her journal with the clean, composed hand of a woman who believed she was being kind.

*I've cleared out the south storeroom. Cold, yes, but quiet. No windows. No mirrors. I informed the staff and children that she was taken in by a remote chapter. Let this be my mercy.*

It was, by Calvra's standards, mercy. Which tells you everything about Calvra's standards.

## Survival — How She Lived

This is the question that has no clean answer, because no one who knew the truth ever wrote it down.

**In the earliest months, it was [[Magda Velt\|Magda Velt]].**

Not out of defiance, not out of any grand maternal instinct — but out of the same compulsion that made her slip second helpings to the skinny ones. She heard something in the storage room. A sound too small and too insistent to ignore. She didn't ask [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Calvra]] about it. She didn't ask anyone. She simply began leaving warm broth inside the door — quietly, before her rounds, before anyone else was awake — and then closing it again and telling herself it wasn't her business.

She convinced herself it was temporary. That someone else was handling it. That the child was fine.

As Nasha grew past infancy, and Calvra's framing of *"that evil girl in the storage room"* calcified into accepted Asylum reality, Magda's visits stopped. The guilt became easier to bury than the door was to open. She never spoke of it. Not to staff. Not to herself, really.

She was the first person to keep Nasha alive. She was also, ultimately, the person who chose to forget her.

**After Magda stepped away, the [[06_NPCs/Faintlings\|Faintlings]] took over.**

Not consciously — Faintlings have no consciousness, no intent, no capacity for decision. But Nasha was the loudest source of raw, unwitnessed suffering in the entire building. She was an infant broadcasting on the exact frequency the Faintlings were made of: pure, unobserved need. They couldn't ignore her any more than water can ignore a drain.

They kept her warm, pressing close to the walls of the storage room in those cold early years. They kept the rats at bay until she was old enough to manage them herself. They may have nudged Calvra — in the same unconscious way they nudge everything in the Asylum — to occasionally check the door, to leave it marginally less sealed than she intended.

And as Nasha grew older, something stranger happened.

She began to absorb them.

Not in any predatory sense — she simply *listened*, the way she listened to everything that came through the walls. The Faintlings spoke constantly in their fragmented, looping tongue. She had no other language to learn. The old forgotten words that began emerging from her mouth were not gibberish. They were Faintling language — the psychic residue dialect of the Asylum's accumulated grief, spoken back to its source by the only living creature who had ever been immersed in it long enough to learn it.

The symbols she etched into the storage room walls with her fingernails were the same. Not random markings. Faintling language, written down.

She was, without knowing it, the only person who had ever spoken to the Faintlings in a language they recognized as their own. Whether they understood this is impossible to say. But they stayed near her door long after any instinct toward warmth or need had been satisfied.

They were, in the only sense available to things without souls, her companions.

## Childhood in the Storage Room

The room had no bed, no toys, no light. Just broken furniture, old linens, rusted tools — and rats. She survived on them. At first out of desperation. Later, with eerie calm.

No one spoke to her. No one taught her language, or her name, or that the world outside the door was anything other than a sound she heard through wood and stone. Yet Nasha learned anyway. She listened through the walls. She absorbed the whispers of the Asylum's children, the fragments of lessons, the lullabies [[Magda Velt\|Magda Velt]] hummed in the kitchen two corridors away — sounds that filtered through plaster and settled in her like sediment. And she listened to the Faintlings, endlessly, until their fragmented tongue became the closest thing she had to a mother tongue.

She stopped standing. Crawled instead, silently. Her body thinned, her nails grew sharp, her skin turned sallow in the dark. She no longer spoke in words — only in murmurs, mutters, and layered, curling tongues that slithered out of her like breath from a crypt.

She had become feral. Not wild like an animal, but something far worse — forgotten, unfinished, unobserved. A thought left alone too long. A prayer buried without a god to hear it.

[[Magda Velt\|Magda Velt]] knew there was a child in the storage room. She heard her, sometimes. She called her "that evil girl" — not out of malice, but because that was [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn's]] framing, absorbed over years of proximity without questioning. By then, Magda had chosen not to remember what she knew.

This is the part of Magda's story that is not gentle.

## The Party's Arrival

By the time [[05_Player Characters/Squeaks\|Squeaks]], [[05_Player Characters/Vespertina\|Vespertina]], [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]], and [[05_Player Characters/Renzo\|Renzo]] reached the storage room, Nasha was a teenager — though she looked younger, her body stunted by years of darkness and malnutrition. She moved on all fours. She did not use language in any recognizable form.

She fought them.

Not with strategy. Not with cruelty. With the pure, unthinking terror of a creature that had never experienced a stranger entering her space without the door being immediately locked again. The party was the first people to open that door and *leave it open*.

She had a strange, feral power — the accumulated psychic residue of the Asylum soaked into her over years, the forgotten tongues she'd taught herself, the symbols that pulsed in the wood. She was dangerous the way a cornered animal is dangerous: entirely, and without any of the satisfaction a villain provides.

As she died, she stumbled backward. Blood ran down her face. She looked at [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]] — not with rage, but with something that had no business being in a feral child's eyes.

Sadness. Abandonment.

Her lip quivered. A single tear ran down her cheek.

> *"But… I said I was sorry."*

She collapsed.

## The Death Burst — Vision of the Candle and the Crate

When Nasha died, the psychic energy she had spent her entire life absorbing released all at once.

A strange pressure built in the air — like the moment before thunder, but deeper, internal. The walls bent inward. Light flickered without a source. A chill crawled behind the eyes of everyone in the room.

Then — **BOOM.**

A silent explosion of smoky purple magic burst outward, consuming everything in a haze of violet ash and static. The party's bodies did not fall. Their minds did — plummeting into a vast, silent dark. Not shadow. A memory.

Every player within 30 feet experienced [[Scene - The Candle and the Crate\|the vision]]: Samantha Virelle, alone in the sub-level of [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Ya'a Thalor\|Ya'a Thalor]], giving birth. The red cloth. The whispered apology. The walk through the dark forest. The doorstep. The kiss. The candle guttering out just as [[06_NPCs/Rhoswyn Calvra\|Rhoswyn Calvra]] opened the door.

The darkness receded like smoke sucked through a keyhole. The air was still thick with violet shimmer, fading like embers. Some of the party were sweating. One was crying. There was a metallic taste in every mouth.

They had just killed her. And now they knew everything.

## The Aftermath — What Nasha's Death Caused

Nasha's death set in motion a chain of consequences that reshaped the campaign.

**[[06_NPCs/Sanctifier Samantha Virelle\|Samantha]]** found her body in the storage room after crawling through the portal from [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Ya'a Thalor\|Ya'a Thalor]] — broken, barely alive, silenced by [[Magda Velt\|Magda's]] soup, and searching desperately for a daughter she had spent years trying to reclaim. She found Nasha on the floor, in the dark, still dressed in the remnants of the red blanket.

She knelt in the filth. She pulled the girl's body into her lap. She rocked her.

She stayed there until the grief curdled into something else.

What rose from that room was not Samantha Virelle. It was the seed of [[06_NPCs/Malis Fiani, The Scarlet Raven\|Malis Fiani, The Scarlet Raven]].

**[[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]]** carried the weight of Nasha's death differently — and it became the central axis of his relationship with Samantha, with [[06_NPCs/Gods/Bahamut\|Bahamut]], and with [[06_NPCs/Gods/Nerull\|Nerull]]'s manipulation. He had made a mercy kill. He had also, without knowing it, destroyed a mother's only reason to keep choosing humanity.

**[[Items/Magical/Nasha's Red Blanket\|Nasha's Red Blanket]]** — the red cloth Samantha had wrapped her in at birth, still with her when she died — became a relic of the tragedy. Samantha took it with her when she left the storage room. She carried it to the Chapel of [[Vex Ceniza\|Vex Ceniza]] when she attempted the resurrection ritual. When that failed, it was the last thing she held over Nasha's grave in [[Vis'Avae\|Vis'Avae]].

The blanket still remembers warmth, according to those who've held it since.

## What Nasha Never Knew

- Her name. It was never spoken to her.
- Her mother had defied a direct order to keep her alive.
- Her mother had spent years in captivity at [[02 World/The Evergrowing Forest/Ya'a Thalor\|Ya'a Thalor]] partly because of the scandal her birth created.
- Her mother had tried to find her.
- [[Magda Velt\|Magda Velt]] had kept her alive as an infant — and then chosen to stop.
- [[Magda Velt\|Magda Velt]] was thirty feet away, on the other side of a wall, for most of her life.
- The symbols she etched into the wood were Faintling language — a psychic dialect no scholar had ever formally documented, learned by a child with no other tongue available.
- The [[06_NPCs/Faintlings\|Faintlings]] that haunted the Asylum's halls had, in the only way available to things without souls, kept her company.
- She had [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier John Halric\|Halric's]] eyes. Silver-gray. Wide.
- She said *"I'm sorry"* before she died. No one knows what she was apologizing for. Possibly everything. Possibly nothing specific. Possibly just for existing in a world that had made it very clear she wasn't supposed to.

## Legacy

Nasha appears twice more after her death, in a sense:

**In [[06_NPCs/Sanctifier Samantha Virelle\|Samantha's]] grief arc** — she is the wound that never closes, the loss that transforms a broken woman into something more and worse than human. Every act of the Scarlet Raven traces back to that storage room floor.

**In [[05_Player Characters/Kovinar\|Kovinar]]'s arc** — she is the test [[06_NPCs/Gods/Nerull\|Nerull]] used against him. The impossible choice framed to look like mercy. The proof that even righteous hands can be made into tools. Samantha later showed Kovinar, through a psychic intrusion, that Nerull had nudged him toward that moment — that the death of Nasha was not Kovinar's sin alone but Nerull's design, executed through a paladin's principled exhaustion.

Whether that absolution is real, or simply what Samantha needed to believe in order to redirect her rage from the party toward the god — is a question the campaign does not answer cleanly.
